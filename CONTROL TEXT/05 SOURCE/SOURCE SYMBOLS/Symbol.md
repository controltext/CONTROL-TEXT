NOTES:  
  
A symbol possesses meaning not because of its physical form but because a collective associates meaning with it. Symbols often act as containers that compress large amounts of information into recognizable forms.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Human civilization operates through symbols. A symbol functions as a meaning-compression mechanism that transforms complex experiences, beliefs, identities, and narratives into transferable forms. Through symbols, cultures preserve memory, institutions preserve authority, religions preserve meaning, nations preserve identity, and language preserves thought. Control Text emerges when symbols become the primary medium through which reality is interpreted, organized, and transmitted.
