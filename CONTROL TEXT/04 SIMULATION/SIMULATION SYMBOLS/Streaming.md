SYSTEMS [[29 DIGITAL RITUALS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A digital process through which content, media, communication, experiences, performances, information, or signals are continuously transmitted and received in real time or near real time without requiring complete prior download or storage.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms content into continuous experience by creating persistent flows of information, entertainment, communication, participation, and attention.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Live streaming  
- Video streaming  
- Music streaming  
- Game streaming  
- Podcast streaming  
- News streaming  
- Virtual event broadcasting

NOTES:  
  
Streaming may be live, recorded, interactive, passive, personalized, or algorithmically curated. It often creates a sense of immediacy, presence, continuity, and participation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Digital environments increasingly favor flow over permanence. Streaming functions as a signal-continuity mechanism that transforms content from isolated objects into ongoing experiences. Through streaming, audiences become continuously connected, attention becomes sustained over time, and participation occurs within living streams rather than static artifacts. Streaming represents the ritualization of presence within digital reality.
