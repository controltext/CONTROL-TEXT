SYSTEMS [[26 REALITY TUNNELS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A social, informational, communicative, or cultural environment in which similar beliefs, narratives, assumptions, perspectives, and interpretations are continuously reinforced through repeated exposure and mutual affirmation.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Amplifies and stabilizes reality tunnels by increasing the frequency of confirming signals while reducing the influence of conflicting signals.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political echo chambers  
- Social media communities  
- Ideological groups  
- Online forums  
- Religious communities  
- Corporate cultures  
- Intellectual circles

NOTES:  
  
Echo chambers may form intentionally or unintentionally. They emerge through shared interests, identities, incentives, social structures, communication patterns, recommendation systems, and selective exposure to information.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Beliefs strengthen when they are repeatedly reflected back to their holders. An echo chamber functions as a reality-reinforcement mechanism that amplifies familiar interpretations through repetition and social confirmation. Through echo chambers, narratives gain perceived certainty, assumptions gain legitimacy, and worldviews become increasingly self-validating. The more often a perspective is echoed, the more natural and obvious it may appear within a reality tunnel.
