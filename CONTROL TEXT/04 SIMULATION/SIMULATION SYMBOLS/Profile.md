SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured representation of an individual composed of attributes, characteristics, preferences, behaviors, affiliations, histories, identifiers, activities, and associated information used to describe, classify, recognize, or understand identity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms complex identity into an organized model by assembling information into a coherent, searchable, interpretable, and manageable representation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media profiles  
- Customer profiles  
- User profiles  
- Employee profiles  
- Consumer profiles  
- Psychological profiles  
- Behavioral profiles

NOTES:  
  
A profile may be self-created, externally constructed, manually assembled, or algorithmically generated. Profiles often contain both explicit information and inferred characteristics.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Modern systems increasingly interact with representations rather than direct individuals. A profile functions as an identity-modeling mechanism that converts a living person into an organized informational structure. Through profiles, identities become searchable, classifiable, comparable, trackable, and actionable. The profile serves as the informational version of the self within larger systems.
