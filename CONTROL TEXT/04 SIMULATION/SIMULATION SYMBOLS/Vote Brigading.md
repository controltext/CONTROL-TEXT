SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The coordinated effort by a group of participants, accounts, communities, or automated systems to collectively influence voting, ranking, rating, recommendation, reputation, visibility, or feedback mechanisms in order to alter perceived consensus or prominence.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms coordinated participation into perceived collective judgment by artificially concentrating voting power within a system.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Coordinated upvoting campaigns  
- Coordinated downvoting campaigns  
- Rating manipulation efforts  
- Review brigading  
- Poll manipulation  
- Reputation attacks  
- Visibility boosting operations

NOTES:  
  
Vote brigading may involve real participants, coordinated communities, automated accounts, or hybrid systems. Its defining characteristic is the concentration of voting behavior in a way that distorts the appearance of independent collective judgment.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Many digital systems rely on aggregated feedback as a signal of value, relevance, popularity, or quality. Vote brigading functions as a reputation-manipulation mechanism that transforms coordination into perceived public evaluation. Through vote brigading, visibility becomes steerable, rankings become influenceable, and social proof becomes susceptible to organized intervention. Artificial consensus emerges when coordinated voting is interpreted as independent collective preference.
