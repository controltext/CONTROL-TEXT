SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A coordinated effort to repeatedly distribute, reinforce, replicate, and amplify a narrative, message, signal, talking point, perception, or interpretation across multiple channels, communities, platforms, accounts, or networks.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms repetition into perceived consensus by causing observers to encounter the same signals from multiple apparent sources.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Narrative amplification campaigns  
- Coordinated media messaging  
- Multi-platform talking point distribution  
- Repeated political messaging  
- Cross-network influence operations  
- Synchronized hashtag campaigns  
- Viral narrative reinforcement

NOTES:  
  
Echo campaigns may involve humans, automated systems, media organizations, communities, advocacy groups, brands, or political actors. Their defining characteristic is repeated reinforcement across multiple points of exposure.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
People often interpret repetition as evidence of importance, legitimacy, familiarity, or truth. An echo campaign functions as a narrative-reinforcement mechanism that transforms repeated exposure into perceived validation. Through echo campaigns, signals appear to emerge from many independent sources, narratives gain familiarity, and familiarity becomes social proof. Artificial consensus emerges when repetition is mistaken for widespread independent agreement.
