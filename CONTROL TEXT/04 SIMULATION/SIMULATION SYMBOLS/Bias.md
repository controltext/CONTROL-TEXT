DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A tendency, predisposition, preference, assumption, inclination, filter, or interpretive distortion that influences perception, judgment, memory, reasoning, decision-making, or evaluation.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Shapes reality perception by directing attention toward certain information, interpretations, patterns, meanings, or conclusions while reducing attention toward others.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Confirmation bias  
- Selection bias  
- Survivorship bias  
- Availability bias  
- Anchoring bias  
- Attribution bias  
- Cultural bias  
- Ideological bias

NOTES:  
  
Bias is not necessarily malicious, irrational, or avoidable. Bias emerges naturally from limited information, experience, attention, identity, memory, culture, incentives, and cognitive constraints. Every reality tunnel contains biases.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Reality contains more information than any observer can process. Bias functions as a perception-selection mechanism that determines which information receives attention and which information remains unnoticed. Through bias, reality becomes simplified into manageable models, but those models may emphasize certain patterns while obscuring others. Every reality tunnel is shaped not only by what it contains, but by what it excludes.
