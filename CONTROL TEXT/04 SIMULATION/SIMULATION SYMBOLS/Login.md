SYSTEMS [[29 DIGITAL RITUALS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A symbolic authentication process through which an individual identifies, verifies, or presents themselves to a digital system in order to gain access to an environment, platform, service, community, account, or network.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms anonymous presence into recognized participation by linking an individual to an identity, profile, account, role, or digital existence within a system.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media logins  
- Email logins  
- Banking logins  
- Gaming logins  
- Platform authentication  
- Community access  
- Single sign-on systems

NOTES:  
  
A login is often treated as a technical action, yet it carries symbolic significance. It marks the transition from outsider to participant and from anonymity to recognized identity within a digital system.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Every ritual begins with entry. A login functions as a digital initiation mechanism that grants access to a specific environment while simultaneously activating a corresponding identity. Through logins, profiles become active, avatars become present, histories become accessible, and participation becomes possible. The login serves as the threshold crossing between the external world and the digital realm.
