DEFINITION:  
  
A foundational framework, model, pattern, assumption structure, or conceptual system that determines how reality is interpreted, organized, understood, and experienced within a given context.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Establishes the boundaries of perception by defining what questions are asked, what explanations are accepted, what possibilities are recognized, and what phenomena are considered meaningful.

Paradigms often operate invisibly because they define the default assumptions through which reality is interpreted. Multiple paradigms may coexist, compete, overlap, evolve, or collapse over time.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
People rarely question the framework from which they are perceiving reality. A paradigm functions as a reality-generation architecture that determines the rules of interpretation before interpretation occurs. Through paradigms, entire fields of possibility become visible or invisible, shaping what can be imagined, believed, perceived, explained, or considered real.
