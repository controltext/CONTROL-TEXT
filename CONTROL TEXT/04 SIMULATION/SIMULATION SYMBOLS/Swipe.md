SYSTEMS [[29 DIGITAL RITUALS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A symbolic digital gesture through which an individual advances, dismisses, selects, evaluates, filters, or navigates content, profiles, signals, images, experiences, or information streams.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms attention into continuous navigation by allowing users to rapidly sort, evaluate, and move through digital environments with minimal effort.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media scrolling  
- Dating app swipes  
- Short-form video navigation  
- Story progression  
- Image browsing  
- Feed navigation  
- Content filtering

NOTES:  
  
A swipe may indicate approval, rejection, curiosity, indifference, exploration, or navigation depending upon context. Modern digital platforms often structure user experience around repeated swiping behaviors.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Digital environments increasingly prioritize speed of interaction. A swipe functions as an attention-navigation mechanism that transforms decision-making into a rapid, repeatable ritual. Through swipes, content becomes sortable, identities become selectable, and attention becomes continuously directed through streams of information. The swipe reduces complex evaluation into simple motion, allowing participation to occur at massive scale and speed.
