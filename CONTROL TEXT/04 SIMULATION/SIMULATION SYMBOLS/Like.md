SYSTEMS [[29 DIGITAL RITUALS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A symbolic digital gesture through which an individual acknowledges, approves of, supports, recognizes, reacts to, or participates in a piece of content, message, image, event, identity, or signal.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms attention into visible participation by allowing individuals to perform lightweight acts of recognition and affiliation within digital environments.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media likes  
- Video likes  
- Post reactions  
- Content appreciation signals  
- Platform engagement actions  
- Community approval signals  
- Digital acknowledgments

NOTES:  
  
A like may express many different meanings depending on context, including appreciation, agreement, support, acknowledgment, curiosity, habit, affiliation, or simple recognition. The meaning of a like is often interpreted through surrounding signals.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Rituals often emerge from repeated symbolic actions. A like functions as a digital affirmation mechanism that transforms attention into visible participation. Through likes, individuals signal presence, acknowledgment, approval, affiliation, and engagement while simultaneously contributing to systems of reputation, visibility, ranking, and social validation. The power of the like lies not in its complexity but in its repeatability.
