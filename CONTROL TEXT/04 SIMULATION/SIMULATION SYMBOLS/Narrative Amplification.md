SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The intentional expansion, reinforcement, distribution, repetition, promotion, or elevation of a narrative, message, interpretation, storyline, framing, belief, perception, or talking point across a network or information environment.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms a narrative into a dominant signal by increasing its visibility, repetition, familiarity, and perceived significance.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Coordinated messaging campaigns  
- Media narrative reinforcement  
- Talking point distribution  
- Hashtag promotion efforts  
- Multi-platform message repetition  
- Influencer amplification campaigns  
- Viral narrative engineering

NOTES:  
  
Narrative amplification may occur organically or through coordination. Within artificial consensus systems, amplification often functions by increasing exposure frequency, creating familiarity, and generating perceptions of importance or legitimacy.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
People frequently interpret visibility as significance. Narrative amplification functions as a narrative-momentum mechanism that transforms repetition into perceived importance. Through amplification, narratives appear larger, more widespread, more urgent, and more legitimate than their underlying adoption may justify. Artificial consensus emerges when amplified narratives are mistaken for broadly held beliefs rather than highly distributed signals.
