SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A collection of automated, semi-automated, centrally coordinated, or algorithmically managed accounts, agents, identities, or nodes that operate together to distribute, amplify, reinforce, replicate, or simulate patterns of activity within a network.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms coordinated activity into perceived consensus by generating signals that create the appearance of widespread participation, popularity, agreement, attention, engagement, or support.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media bot networks  
- Automated engagement networks  
- Coordinated amplification systems  
- Synthetic follower networks  
- Automated repost systems  
- Content distribution bots  
- Simulated community activity

NOTES:  
  
Bot networks may be used for marketing, automation, customer support, content distribution, data collection, social simulation, audience growth, political influence, spam, or information operations. Their defining characteristic is coordinated activity rather than individual autonomy.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Perceptions of popularity often emerge from visible activity rather than direct measurement of belief. A bot network functions as a consensus-simulation mechanism that converts coordination into perceived participation. Through bot networks, engagement becomes reproducible, visibility becomes scalable, and signals acquire the appearance of social validation. Artificial consensus emerges when observers interpret coordinated activity as evidence of independent agreement.
