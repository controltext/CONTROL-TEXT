SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A constructed, projected, performed, presented, or socially expressed identity through which an individual is perceived, recognized, interpreted, or understood by others.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms identity into a recognizable social interface by organizing behaviors, symbols, appearances, narratives, roles, and expressions into a coherent public presentation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Professional personas  
- Online personas  
- Creator personas  
- Celebrity personas  
- Brand personas  
- Political personas  
- Social personas

NOTES:  
  
A persona is not necessarily false or deceptive. It is the organized presentation of identity within a particular context. Individuals may maintain multiple personas simultaneously across different environments and social systems.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Identity is often experienced internally but recognized externally. A persona functions as an identity-interface mechanism that translates internal experience into socially understandable forms. Through personas, individuals become recognizable characters within social systems, allowing identity to be communicated, interpreted, evaluated, categorized, and remembered.
