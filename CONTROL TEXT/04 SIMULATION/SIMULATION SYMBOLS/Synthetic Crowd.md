SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A simulated, manufactured, artificially amplified, or partially fabricated population of participants that creates the appearance of a larger, more active, more engaged, or more unified collective than actually exists.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms coordinated signals into perceived mass participation by creating the appearance of a crowd through artificial amplification, identity multiplication, automation, repetition, or manufactured engagement.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Bot-generated audiences  
- Inflated follower communities  
- Artificial engagement populations  
- Manufactured support movements  
- Simulated online communities  
- Coordinated amplification networks  
- Synthetic participation campaigns

NOTES:  
  
A synthetic crowd may consist of automated accounts, duplicate identities, coordinated participants, inflated metrics, manufactured engagement, or mixtures of authentic and artificial activity. Its defining characteristic is the creation of perceived scale beyond underlying participation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Human beings frequently interpret numbers as evidence of legitimacy. A synthetic crowd functions as a mass-participation simulation mechanism that transforms coordination into apparent population. Through synthetic crowds, visibility becomes magnitude, engagement becomes community, and repetition becomes perceived collective will. Artificial consensus emerges when the appearance of a crowd is mistaken for the independent decisions of many individuals.
