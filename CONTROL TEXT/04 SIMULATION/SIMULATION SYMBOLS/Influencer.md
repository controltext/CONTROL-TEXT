SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual whose identity is substantially linked to the generation, distribution, amplification, or shaping of attention, opinions, preferences, behaviors, trends, narratives, or cultural signals within a network or audience.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms visibility and audience attention into social influence by linking personal identity to signal production, audience engagement, reputation, and persuasion.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media influencers  
- Content creators  
- Streamers  
- Public personalities  
- Industry thought leaders  
- Lifestyle influencers  
- Cultural trendsetters

NOTES:  
  
Influence may arise from expertise, entertainment, status, trust, charisma, authority, visibility, community participation, or cultural relevance. Influence can exist at scales ranging from small communities to global audiences.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention can become a source of social power. An influencer functions as an identity-amplification mechanism that converts visibility into influence. Through influencer identity, audiences become communities, signals become cultural forces, and personal identity becomes intertwined with reputation, reach, engagement, and the ability to shape the perceptions and behaviors of others.
