SYSTEMS [[31 REALITY MARKETS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The act of risking, staking, committing, or allocating resources based upon an expectation, prediction, probability assessment, forecast, or belief regarding a future outcome.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms belief into exposure by linking expectations about future reality to potential gains, losses, rewards, or consequences.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Sports betting  
- Election betting  
- Financial speculation  
- Wagers  
- Prediction contests  
- Outcome forecasting markets  
- Competitive gaming bets

NOTES:  
  
Betting may occur formally through markets and institutions or informally through personal agreements. The defining characteristic is the linking of belief to consequence through risk.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Beliefs become more visible when individuals are willing to incur consequences for them. Betting functions as a belief-exposure mechanism that transforms expectations into commitments. Through betting, uncertainty becomes measurable, confidence becomes quantifiable, and forecasts become actionable. Reality markets emerge when future possibilities become objects of risk, speculation, and exchange.
