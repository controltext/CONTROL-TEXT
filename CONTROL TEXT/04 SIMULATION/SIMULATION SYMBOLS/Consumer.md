SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual whose role within a social, economic, cultural, or informational system involves the selection, acquisition, use, consumption, evaluation, or adoption of goods, services, experiences, media, symbols, or brands.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms identity into a pattern of preferences, choices, purchases, affiliations, and consumption behaviors that can be observed, categorized, influenced, and modeled.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Product consumers  
- Media consumers  
- Entertainment consumers  
- Technology consumers  
- Luxury consumers  
- Lifestyle consumers  
- Brand consumers

NOTES:  
  
The term consumer describes a functional role within a system and does not encompass the entirety of an individual's identity. Individuals simultaneously occupy many identities beyond consumption-related roles.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Identity can be expressed through patterns of acquisition and preference. A consumer functions as an identity-classification mechanism that links choices, behaviors, interests, and affiliations to broader social and economic systems. Through consumer identity, preferences become signals, purchases become expressions, and patterns of consumption become part of how individuals are categorized, modeled, and understood.
