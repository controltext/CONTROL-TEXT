SYSTEMS [[29 DIGITAL RITUALS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A symbolic digital action through which an individual redistributes, retransmits, republishes, forwards, amplifies, or propagates content, information, narratives, identities, or signals to additional audiences.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms private attention into public transmission by allowing signals to move beyond their original audience and enter new social environments.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media shares  
- Reposts  
- Retweets  
- Forwarded messages  
- Content redistribution  
- Story sharing  
- Link sharing

NOTES:  
  
Sharing may communicate approval, disagreement, curiosity, humor, identity, affiliation, urgency, usefulness, or simple visibility. The act of sharing often carries social meaning independent of the content itself.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information spreads through participation. A share functions as a signal-amplification mechanism that converts individual attention into network transmission. Through shares, audiences become distributors, consumers become broadcasters, and content gains the ability to move across communities, platforms, identities, and social boundaries. Sharing is one of the primary rituals through which digital realities expand.
