DEFINITION:  
  
A comprehensive framework of assumptions, beliefs, values, interpretations, expectations, narratives, and explanatory models through which an individual or group understands reality, existence, society, nature, identity, and experience.  
  
FUNCTION:

NOTES:  
  
A worldview is broader than a single belief. It represents the larger structure within which individual beliefs acquire coherence, meaning, and relationship to one another.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Individual beliefs rarely exist in isolation. A worldview functions as a reality-modeling architecture that organizes beliefs into a coherent simulation of existence. Through worldviews, people construct explanations for who they are, what reality is, how events occur, what is valuable, what is possible, and what the future may contain. Different worldviews generate different reality tunnels even when observing the same world.
