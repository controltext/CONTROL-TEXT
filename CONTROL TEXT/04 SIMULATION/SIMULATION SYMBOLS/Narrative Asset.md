SYSTEMS [[31 REALITY MARKETS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A narrative, story, belief, interpretation, vision, expectation, identity, myth, or conceptual framework that possesses value due to its capacity to influence perception, decision-making, attention, coordination, valuation, or future behavior.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms meaning into value by allowing narratives to operate as assets that shape expectations regarding future reality.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Growth narratives  
- Brand narratives  
- Technological narratives  
- Investment theses  
- Political narratives  
- Cultural myths  
- Innovation stories  
- Economic narratives

NOTES:  
  
Narrative assets derive value not from physical properties but from their ability to organize attention, expectations, investment, participation, trust, coordination, and collective behavior.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Markets frequently respond not only to material conditions but to stories about material conditions. A narrative asset functions as a meaning-valuation mechanism that transforms interpretation into economic and social value. Through narrative assets, beliefs become investments, stories become market forces, and expectations become self-reinforcing signals. Reality markets emerge when narratives themselves become tradeable drivers of future outcomes.
