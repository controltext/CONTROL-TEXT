SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An organized system, network, operation, or coordinated group that generates engagement signals such as likes, comments, shares, follows, reactions, ratings, views, or interactions for the purpose of increasing perceived popularity, visibility, influence, legitimacy, or consensus.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms coordinated interaction into perceived public interest by manufacturing engagement signals that appear to originate from independent participants.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Like farms  
- Click farms  
- Follower farms  
- Comment farms  
- View farms  
- Rating farms  
- Coordinated engagement networks

NOTES:  
  
Engagement farms may involve automated systems, human participants, outsourced labor, coordinated communities, or hybrid arrangements. Their defining characteristic is the systematic production of engagement signals rather than organic participation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Many digital systems interpret engagement as evidence of value, relevance, popularity, or legitimacy. An engagement farm functions as a social-proof manufacturing mechanism that converts coordinated interaction into perceived public interest. Through engagement farms, visibility can be amplified, reputations can be elevated, and popularity can appear larger than underlying participation would otherwise suggest. Artificial consensus emerges when manufactured engagement is interpreted as authentic collective response.
