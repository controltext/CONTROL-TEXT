NOTES:  
  
Nations are sustained not merely by geography or government but by shared stories, symbols, identities, and collective memory. National identity often persists through generations despite changes in leadership, institutions, or territory.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Collective identities seek scale. A nation functions as a large-scale identity-coordination mechanism that transforms millions of individuals into a perceived collective self. Through nations, myths become history, symbols become loyalties, institutions become legitimacy, and cultures become civilizations. Within the Egregore Network, nations represent some of the most powerful collective entities ever created, capable of organizing belief, behavior, sacrifice, cooperation, and purpose across vast populations and long periods of time.
