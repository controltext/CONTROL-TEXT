SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A fabricated, deceptive, manipulated, purchased, coordinated, misleading, or non-authentic evaluation presented as an independent review, rating, testimonial, recommendation, or assessment.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms manufactured opinion into perceived public judgment by creating the appearance of authentic individual evaluation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Fabricated product reviews  
- Purchased testimonials  
- Coordinated review campaigns  
- Fake ratings  
- Synthetic customer feedback  
- Reputation manipulation efforts  
- Review farm submissions

NOTES:  
  
Fake reviews may be positive, negative, promotional, competitive, automated, coordinated, purchased, or fabricated. Their defining characteristic is the presentation of non-authentic evaluation as independent judgment.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
People often trust the experiences and opinions of others when making decisions. A fake review functions as a trust-simulation mechanism that transforms manufactured evaluation into perceived public judgment. Through fake reviews, reputation becomes engineerable, legitimacy becomes producible, and trust becomes influenceable. Artificial consensus emerges when fabricated evaluations are interpreted as authentic collective experience.
