SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A concealed, alternate, secondary, or deceptive identity operated by an individual or organization for the purpose of participating in discussions, influencing perceptions, amplifying messages, supporting positions, attacking opponents, or creating the appearance of independent participation.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms centralized control into perceived plurality by allowing a single actor to appear as multiple independent participants within a network.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Alternate forum identities  
- Anonymous influence accounts  
- Hidden advocacy accounts  
- Multiple social media personas  
- Disguised promotional accounts  
- Secret support accounts  
- Coordinated identity networks

NOTES:  
  
A sock puppet differs from a generic fake account because it is typically controlled by an existing actor who conceals their connection to the alternate identity. The primary purpose is often to create the appearance of independent agreement or participation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
People frequently evaluate ideas based upon the number of seemingly independent voices supporting them. A sock puppet functions as an identity-multiplication mechanism that transforms one participant into many apparent participants. Through sock puppets, agreement can appear broader, narratives can seem reinforced, and conversations can appear more diverse than their actual origins. Artificial consensus emerges when multiple apparent voices originate from a single hidden source.
