DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A symbolic, visual, digital, conceptual, narrative, or representative embodiment through which an individual expresses, projects, navigates, or performs identity within a particular environment or system.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms identity into a usable representation by creating a proxy through which an individual can participate, communicate, interact, and be recognized within a simulated space.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Video game avatars  
- Social media avatars  
- Virtual reality avatars  
- Online personas  
- Character identities  
- Digital representations  
- Symbolic self-representations

NOTES:  
  
An avatar may closely resemble an individual's self-concept or may differ significantly from it. Avatars can be visual, textual, symbolic, fictional, aspirational, or functional. Multiple avatars may coexist simultaneously across different environments.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Identity often requires a vessel through which it can operate. An avatar functions as an identity-embodiment mechanism that allows individuals to project themselves into environments where direct presence is impossible, impractical, or unnecessary. Through avatars, identity becomes portable, adaptable, and capable of existing within multiple realities simultaneously. The avatar serves as the operative self inside the simulation.
