SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual who develops a sustained emotional, symbolic, cultural, social, or personal attachment to a person, group, creator, team, brand, movement, work, community, or phenomenon.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms preference into identity by linking admiration, loyalty, participation, and belonging to specific symbols, personalities, groups, or cultural objects.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Sports fans  
- Music fans  
- Movie fans  
- Creator fans  
- Brand fans  
- Gaming fans  
- Community fans

NOTES:  
  
A fan identity often extends beyond consumption into participation, community membership, emotional investment, symbolic affiliation, and self-expression. Fans may form communities, rituals, traditions, and shared narratives around the object of their attention.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention can evolve into attachment. A fan functions as an identity-formation mechanism that transforms admiration and repeated engagement into belonging and affiliation. Through fan identity, preferences become communities, interests become loyalties, and external symbols become integrated into personal identity.
