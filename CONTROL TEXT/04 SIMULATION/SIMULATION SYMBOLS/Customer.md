SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual recognized through an ongoing relationship with a business, service provider, organization, platform, institution, or commercial system involving purchases, transactions, subscriptions, memberships, or recurring exchanges of value.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms transactions into relationships by linking individuals to systems of service, loyalty, support, retention, and commercial identity.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Retail customers  
- Subscription customers  
- Banking customers  
- Telecommunications customers  
- Software customers  
- Hospitality customers  
- Platform customers

NOTES:  
  
A customer differs from a consumer in that customer identity often implies an ongoing relationship, while consumption may occur as a single event. Customers are frequently classified through loyalty, purchasing history, engagement, retention, and lifetime value.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Modern systems increasingly organize identity through relationships rather than isolated transactions. A customer functions as a relationship-assignment mechanism that connects individuals to brands, services, institutions, and platforms over time. Through customer identity, transactions become histories, preferences become profiles, and recurring interactions become part of how individuals are categorized and understood.
