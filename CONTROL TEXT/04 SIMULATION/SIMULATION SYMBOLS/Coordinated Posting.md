SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The deliberate synchronization of content publication, messaging, narratives, talking points, signals, or communications across multiple accounts, identities, participants, organizations, or networks to maximize visibility, amplification, consistency, and perceived consensus.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms organized communication into perceived widespread participation by creating simultaneous or patterned distributions of similar signals across a network.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Coordinated social media campaigns  
- Synchronized messaging efforts  
- Organized hashtag campaigns  
- Narrative amplification operations  
- Multi-account posting initiatives  
- Political communication campaigns  
- Brand promotion networks

NOTES:  
  
Coordinated posting may involve automated systems, human participants, organizations, communities, marketing teams, advocacy groups, or political actors. Coordination itself is not inherently deceptive; its role within artificial consensus depends upon how it is presented and perceived.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Repetition across multiple sources often creates perceptions of legitimacy and importance. Coordinated posting functions as a signal-synchronization mechanism that transforms organization into visibility. Through coordinated posting, narratives appear simultaneously across networks, messages gain reinforcement, and observers encounter repeated signals that may appear to originate independently. Artificial consensus emerges when synchronization is perceived as spontaneous agreement.
