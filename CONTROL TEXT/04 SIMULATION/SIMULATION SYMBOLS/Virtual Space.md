SYSTEMS [[28 HYPER REALITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A digitally constructed, symbolically represented, computationally generated, or simulated environment within which identities, objects, interactions, experiences, communications, and activities can occur independently of direct physical space.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms representation into environment by creating a domain in which simulations, avatars, symbols, narratives, and interactions can exist as operational realities.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Virtual worlds  
- Online communities  
- Social media platforms  
- Metaverse environments  
- Video game worlds  
- Virtual reality spaces  
- Digital collaboration environments

NOTES:  
  
Virtual spaces may range from simple communication environments to highly immersive simulated worlds. Their significance derives from the interactions, meanings, identities, and experiences they support rather than their physical existence.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Representations become increasingly powerful when they acquire environments of their own. A virtual space functions as a reality-hosting mechanism that allows symbols, identities, simulations, and interactions to operate within self-contained domains. Through virtual spaces, experiences become detached from physical geography, identities become portable, and simulations become inhabitable rather than merely observable.
