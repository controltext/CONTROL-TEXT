SYSTEMS [[30 ARTIFICIAL CONSENSUS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The coordinated creation, amplification, or simulation of apparent grassroots participation, support, opposition, consensus, activism, engagement, or public sentiment in order to create the perception of organic collective opinion.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms coordination into perceived spontaneity by making organized activity appear as independent, widespread, and naturally occurring public behavior.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Manufactured grassroots campaigns  
- Coordinated online advocacy  
- Simulated public support movements  
- Artificial community engagement  
- Organized review campaigns  
- Coordinated social media participation  
- Synthetic public opinion campaigns

NOTES:  
  
Astroturfing may involve human participants, automated systems, coordinated messaging, organized campaigns, synthetic engagement, or combinations thereof. Its defining characteristic is the appearance of grassroots activity despite underlying coordination.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
People often judge ideas by observing the apparent behavior of others. Astroturfing functions as a consensus-fabrication mechanism that transforms organized activity into perceived public sentiment. Through astroturfing, coordination becomes visibility, visibility becomes social proof, and social proof becomes perceived legitimacy. Artificial consensus emerges when observers mistake orchestrated participation for independent collective agreement.
