SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual identified through a formal role, position, function, occupation, or set of responsibilities within an organization, institution, company, agency, enterprise, or collective system of work.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms labor, skills, responsibilities, and participation into a structured social identity that can be classified, managed, evaluated, coordinated, and integrated into larger organizational systems.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Corporate employees  
- Government employees  
- Retail employees  
- Factory employees  
- Healthcare employees  
- Technology employees  
- Service industry employees

NOTES:  
  
Employee identity often extends beyond the workplace itself. Professional roles may influence social status, self-concept, community membership, personal narratives, schedules, behaviors, values, and life structure.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Modern institutions frequently organize identity through function. An employee functions as a role-assignment mechanism that links individuals to structured systems of responsibility, productivity, hierarchy, and coordination. Through employee identity, tasks become occupations, occupations become social categories, and participation within organizations becomes part of personal identity.
