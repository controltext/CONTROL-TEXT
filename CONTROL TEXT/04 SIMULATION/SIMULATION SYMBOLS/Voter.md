SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual identified as a participant within a political, organizational, social, or governance system through the expression of preferences, choices, endorsements, selections, or decisions regarding leadership, policy, representation, or collective action.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms political preferences and affiliations into an identity category that can be recognized, classified, analyzed, influenced, organized, and mobilized.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Registered voters  
- Party-affiliated voters  
- Independent voters  
- Swing voters  
- Issue-based voters  
- Local election voters  
- National election voters

NOTES:  
  
The identity of a voter extends beyond the act of voting itself. It may involve political affiliation, civic participation, ideological identification, issue prioritization, and social group membership.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Identity can be organized through participation in collective decision systems. A voter functions as an identity-classification mechanism that links political preferences, affiliations, priorities, and behaviors to larger governance structures. Through voter identity, individuals become segments, constituencies, demographics, and political categories capable of being analyzed, addressed, persuaded, mobilized, and represented.
