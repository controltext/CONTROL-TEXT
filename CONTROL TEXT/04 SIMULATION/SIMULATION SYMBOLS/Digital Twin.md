SYSTEMS [[28 HYPER REALITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A virtual, computational, informational, or simulated representation of a real-world object, person, system, environment, process, organization, or phenomenon that mirrors, models, tracks, or reflects aspects of its physical counterpart.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms reality into a continuously representable model by creating a parallel version capable of observation, analysis, prediction, simulation, and interaction.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Industrial digital twins  
- Smart city models  
- Digital human models  
- Infrastructure simulations  
- Vehicle digital twins  
- Building information models  
- Organizational simulations

NOTES:  
  
Unlike a static copy or replica, a digital twin often changes alongside its counterpart. It may incorporate observations, measurements, behaviors, events, or system states to maintain an ongoing relationship with the thing it represents.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Hyperreality reaches a new stage when representations become operational companions to reality. A digital twin functions as a reality-mirroring mechanism that creates a parallel version of a thing capable of being observed, modeled, analyzed, predicted, and manipulated. Through digital twins, representations cease being merely descriptive and become active participants in decision-making, forecasting, optimization, and simulation.
