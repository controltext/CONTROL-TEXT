SYSTEMS [[28 HYPER REALITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A synthetic or artificially generated representation designed to imitate the appearance, voice, expressions, behaviors, actions, identity, or presence of a real or imagined individual with a high degree of realism.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms simulation into perceived authenticity by creating representations that can convincingly substitute for direct evidence, observation, memory, or recorded reality.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Synthetic video performances  
- AI-generated voices  
- Face-swapped media  
- Digital actor recreations  
- Synthetic interviews  
- Artificial identity simulations  
- Generated character performances

NOTES:  
  
Deepfakes may be used for entertainment, education, artistic expression, simulation, impersonation, misinformation, historical reconstruction, or identity experimentation. Their significance lies in their ability to blur distinctions between recorded reality and generated reality.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Hyperreality intensifies when representations become indistinguishable from their sources. A deepfake functions as an authenticity-simulation mechanism that allows artificial representations to inherit the appearance of reality. Through deepfakes, evidence becomes uncertain, identity becomes reproducible, presence becomes synthetic, and representations acquire the power to compete directly with authentic observations.
