SYSTEMS [[28 HYPER REALITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An appearance, perception, impression, experience, or representation that differs from the underlying reality it seems to depict, creating a discrepancy between what is perceived and what exists.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms perception by generating convincing appearances capable of influencing experience independently of underlying conditions.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Optical illusions  
- Virtual experiences  
- Staged environments  
- Synthetic authenticity  
- Visual effects  
- Social illusions  
- Identity illusions

NOTES:  
  
An illusion is not necessarily falsehood or deception. Illusions may emerge through perception, context, interpretation, expectation, representation, or symbolic construction. Their significance lies in their ability to shape experience regardless of underlying reality.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Experience is often guided more by appearances than by direct access to reality. An illusion functions as a perception-substitution mechanism that allows appearances to influence understanding independently of what is actually present. Through illusions, representations acquire experiential power, symbols acquire emotional reality, and perceptions become capable of competing with direct observation. Hyperreality emerges when illusions become more influential than the realities from which they originated.
