SYSTEMS [[26 REALITY TUNNELS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured collection of assumptions, convictions, principles, interpretations, expectations, narratives, values, and explanatory models through which an individual or group understands, filters, and experiences reality.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms raw experience into coherent meaning by providing interpretive frameworks that determine how events, information, symbols, and observations are perceived and understood.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Religious belief systems  
- Scientific worldviews  
- Political ideologies  
- Philosophical frameworks  
- Cultural assumptions  
- Personal life philosophies  
- Metaphysical models

NOTES:  
  
Belief systems may be explicit or implicit, conscious or unconscious, individual or collective. Multiple belief systems can coexist, overlap, compete, or evolve over time.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Reality is not experienced directly in a purely raw form. Belief systems function as interpretive architectures that organize perception into coherent worlds. Through belief systems, identical events may generate radically different meanings, explanations, expectations, emotions, and conclusions. A belief system does not merely describe reality—it participates in constructing the reality tunnel through which reality is experienced.
