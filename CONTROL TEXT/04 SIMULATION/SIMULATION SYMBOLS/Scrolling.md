SYSTEMS [[29 DIGITAL RITUALS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A symbolic digital action through which an individual continuously navigates, explores, consumes, filters, or traverses streams of content, information, images, signals, narratives, experiences, or communications.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms attention into flow by allowing individuals to move continuously through dynamic information environments.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media scrolling  
- News feed browsing  
- Infinite scroll interfaces  
- Content feed navigation  
- Timeline exploration  
- Forum browsing  
- Content discovery streams

NOTES:  
  
Scrolling may be intentional, exploratory, habitual, informational, recreational, or automatic. Modern digital environments are frequently designed around endless scrolling mechanisms that encourage continuous interaction.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Digital reality increasingly presents itself as a stream rather than a collection of discrete objects. Scrolling functions as an attention-navigation mechanism that transforms information consumption into continuous movement. Through scrolling, signals become flows, content becomes environments, and attention becomes a journey through an ever-renewing landscape of representations. Scrolling is one of the defining rituals through which individuals experience digital reality.
