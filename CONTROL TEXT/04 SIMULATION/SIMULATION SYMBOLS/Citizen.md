SYSTEMS [[27 MANUFACTURED IDENTITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual recognized as a member of a political, civic, legal, cultural, or social community, possessing a defined relationship to a collective system through rights, responsibilities, protections, obligations, participation, and belonging.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms membership within a collective into a recognized identity by linking individuals to institutions, laws, communities, territories, and systems of governance.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- National citizens  
- Municipal citizens  
- Civic participants  
- Community members  
- Legal citizens  
- Dual citizens  
- Digital citizens

CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Large societies require mechanisms for organizing belonging. A citizen functions as a membership-assignment mechanism that links individuals to collective systems of identity, governance, responsibility, and participation. Through citizen identity, individuals become recognized members of larger social structures, connecting personal identity to collective existence.
