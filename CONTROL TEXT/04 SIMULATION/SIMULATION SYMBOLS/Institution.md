NOTES:  
  
Institutions often outlive the individuals who create them. Their persistence depends upon ongoing participation, belief, legitimacy, and symbolic continuity rather than any single person.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Collective minds seek stability. An institution functions as a belief-preservation mechanism that transforms shared ideas into durable systems of organization. Through institutions, myths become structures, values become rules, identities become roles, and collective intentions acquire continuity across generations. The Egregore Network emerges when institutions begin acting as living containers for collective belief, capable of influencing individuals long after their original creators have disappeared.
