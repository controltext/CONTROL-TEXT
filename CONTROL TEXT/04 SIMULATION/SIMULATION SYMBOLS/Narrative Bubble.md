SYSTEMS [[26 REALITY TUNNELS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A bounded informational environment in which a limited set of narratives, interpretations, assumptions, perspectives, explanations, and meanings are continuously reinforced, while alternative narratives receive reduced exposure or consideration.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Stabilizes a reality tunnel by repeatedly exposing observers to compatible narratives and reducing encounters with contradictory interpretations.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political media bubbles  
- Ideological communities  
- Social media information bubbles  
- Religious narrative environments  
- Corporate cultures  
- Academic schools of thought  
- Online communities

NOTES:  
  
Narrative bubbles are not necessarily intentional or deceptive. They often emerge naturally through shared interests, social networks, cultural norms, recommendation systems, incentives, identities, and communication patterns.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Reality is often experienced through recurring stories. A narrative bubble functions as a reality-stabilization mechanism that reinforces specific interpretations through repetition and selective exposure. Through narrative bubbles, certain explanations become familiar, assumptions become normalized, and worldviews become self-confirming. Over time, the bubble itself may become difficult to perceive because it defines the informational boundaries of what feels obvious, reasonable, or true.
