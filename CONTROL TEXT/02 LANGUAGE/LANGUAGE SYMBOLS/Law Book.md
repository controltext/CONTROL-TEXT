DEFINITION:  
  
A written collection of laws, rules, regulations, legal principles, or codified standards preserved within an authoritative textual framework.

Provides a reference source through which legal expectations, rights, responsibilities, procedures, and standards are recorded, preserved, interpreted, and communicated.

- Statutory compilations

NOTES:  
  
Law books preserve legal frameworks by transforming rules and principles into durable textual forms. They serve as reference documents through which legal systems maintain consistency, continuity, interpretation, and institutional memory across time.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Authority often becomes more stable when it is recorded. A law book transforms rules from temporary instructions into enduring textual references, allowing legal frameworks to persist beyond individual leaders, institutions, or generations.
