SYSTEM [[07 SEMANTIC WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The substitution of a mild, indirect, or socially acceptable term in place of a word or phrase that may be perceived as harsh, unpleasant, controversial, or emotionally charged.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Modifies perception by altering the emotional, social, or moral impact associated with a concept while preserving its underlying reference.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- "Collateral Damage" instead of civilian deaths  
- "Downsizing" instead of layoffs  
- "Enhanced Interrogation" instead of torture  
- "Correctional Facility" instead of prison  
- "Passed Away" instead of died  
- "Economic Adjustment" instead of austerity

NOTES:  
  
Euphemisms can reduce emotional resistance, soften public perception, increase social acceptability, or obscure uncomfortable realities. Their effectiveness depends on the audience accepting the substituted language.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Language can alter emotional response without altering underlying reality. A change in terminology can influence how an event, action, or institution is perceived even when the underlying facts remain unchanged.
