SYSTEMS [[15 THE NAME MACHINE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A classification category used to identify and distinguish groups of living organisms according to shared characteristics, biological relationships, and taxonomic criteria.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides a naming framework that organizes biological diversity into recognizable categories, allowing organisms to be identified, classified, compared, and studied.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Human  
- Wolf  
- Oak Tree  
- Bald Eagle  
- Honey Bee  
- Salmon  
- Tiger

NOTES:  
  
Species function as fundamental units within biological classification systems. They allow living organisms to be organized into structured taxonomies that support communication, research, identification, and understanding of biological relationships.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Naming systems often begin by dividing reality into categories. Species represent one of the most widely recognized examples of classification, demonstrating how taxonomies transform continuous biological variation into identifiable groups that can be named, studied, and communicated across generations.
