SYSTEM [[16 THE SPELL FACTORY]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A statement, rule, declaration, or directive that forbids, restricts, prevents, or denies a particular action, behavior, access, practice, or activity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Uses language to establish boundaries by defining what is not permitted, acceptable, authorized, or allowed within a system.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Legal prohibitions  
- Organizational restrictions  
- Rules and regulations  
- Religious prohibitions  
- Safety restrictions  
- Access limitations  
- Behavioral prohibitions

NOTES:  
  
Prohibitions derive their effectiveness from recognized authority, social agreement, institutional enforcement, cultural norms, legal frameworks, or collective acceptance. They may be explicit or implicit, temporary or permanent, broad or specific.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Language can alter behavior not only by directing action but by limiting possibility. A prohibition functions as a performative speech act that establishes boundaries around acceptable conduct, transforming words into constraints that shape choices, behavior, and social order.
