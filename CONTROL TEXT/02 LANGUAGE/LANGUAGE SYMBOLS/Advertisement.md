DEFINITION:  
  
A message, communication, or symbolic presentation designed to attract attention and influence awareness, perception, interest, preference, decision-making, or behavior regarding a person, product, service, idea, organization, or activity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Uses language, symbols, images, narratives, and repetition to shape attention and encourage specific thoughts, emotions, choices, or actions.

- Political advertisements

NOTES:  
  
Advertisements often combine multiple communication techniques, including repetition, emotional appeal, symbolism, social proof, incentives, narratives, and identity association. Their effectiveness may depend upon audience perception, relevance, frequency, and context.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Language can be designed to influence behavior indirectly. Unlike a command, which directly instructs action, an advertisement attempts to shape attention, desire, belief, or preference in ways that increase the likelihood of a chosen outcome. It functions as a persuasive communication structure that seeks to alter future decisions through symbolic influence.
