SYSTEM [[13 TIME CONSTRUCTION]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured system for organizing, measuring, tracking, and communicating time through recurring units, dates, periods, and cycles.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides a shared framework for coordinating activities, recording events, establishing schedules, preserving history, and synchronizing collective behavior across individuals and groups.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Gregorian calendar  
- Lunar calendars  
- Religious calendars  
- Academic calendars  
- Fiscal calendars  
- Event calendars  
- Seasonal calendars

NOTES:  
  
Calendars divide continuous time into recognizable units such as days, weeks, months, seasons, and years. Different cultures and societies may construct and maintain different calendar systems based upon astronomical, religious, historical, administrative, or cultural considerations.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Time becomes socially usable when it is organized into shared structures. Calendars transform the continuous passage of time into a collective framework that allows groups to coordinate behavior, preserve memory, establish routines, and define historical continuity.
