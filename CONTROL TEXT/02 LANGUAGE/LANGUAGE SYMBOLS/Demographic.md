DEFINITION:  
  
A population category used to classify individuals or groups according to shared characteristics, attributes, traits, conditions, or statistical identifiers.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Organizes populations into identifiable categories that can be measured, compared, analyzed, described, and communicated within classification systems.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Age groups  
- Income brackets  
- Educational levels  
- Geographic populations  
- Occupational groups  
- Household types  
- Consumer segments

NOTES:  
  
Demographics simplify large populations into recognizable groups for analysis, communication, planning, organization, and decision-making. Different demographic systems may emphasize different characteristics depending on purpose and context.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Large populations become easier to analyze when divided into categories. Demographics function as naming structures that transform unique individuals into identifiable population groups, allowing institutions, organizations, and systems to organize, interpret, and communicate information about collective human behavior.
