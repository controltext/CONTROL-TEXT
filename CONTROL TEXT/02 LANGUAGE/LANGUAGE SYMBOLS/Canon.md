DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A recognized collection of texts, works, teachings, records, principles, or materials that are accepted as authoritative, legitimate, foundational, or officially recognized within a tradition, institution, discipline, or community.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Establishes boundaries of authority by defining which texts, ideas, narratives, teachings, or records are considered valid sources of reference, interpretation, and transmission.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Religious canons  
- Literary canons  
- Academic canons

NOTES:  
  
Canons define what is included and excluded within a recognized body of knowledge or authority. They often emerge through institutional recognition, tradition, collective agreement, cultural influence, or historical development.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Authority is often established through selection. A canon functions as a filtering mechanism that determines which texts, narratives, teachings, and records are preserved, referenced, transmitted, and regarded as foundational within a system of meaning.
