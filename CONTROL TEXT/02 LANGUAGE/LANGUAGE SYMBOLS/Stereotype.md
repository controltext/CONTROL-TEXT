SYSTEMS [[15 THE NAME MACHINE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A generalized set of characteristics, traits, behaviors, attributes, or expectations associated with a category, group, identity, or classification.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Simplifies complex individuals, groups, or phenomena by attaching commonly recognized assumptions to a category, making interpretation and recognition faster but often reducing nuance and variation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Occupational stereotypes  
- National stereotypes  
- Age-based stereotypes  
- Cultural stereotypes  
- Gender stereotypes  
- Political stereotypes  
- Social class stereotypes

NOTES:  
  
Stereotypes emerge when repeated associations become attached to categories. They may be accurate for some members of a group, inaccurate for others, partially descriptive, exaggerated, oversimplified, positive, negative, or neutral depending on context.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Classification often extends beyond naming into expectation. Once a category is established, assumptions may accumulate around it. Stereotypes function as compressed meaning structures that attach predicted characteristics to classifications, allowing rapid interpretation while potentially reducing the complexity of the individuals or phenomena being categorized.
