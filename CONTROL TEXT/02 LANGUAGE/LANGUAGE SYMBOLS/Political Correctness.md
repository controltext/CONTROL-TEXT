SYSTEM [[07 SEMANTIC WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The practice of using language that seeks to avoid expressions perceived as offensive, exclusionary, discriminatory, or socially unacceptable toward particular groups or identities.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Shapes communication by establishing linguistic norms that influence which terms, labels, descriptions, and expressions are considered appropriate within a given social context.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Inclusive language guidelines  
- Preferred terminology  
- Institutional communication policies  
- Sensitivity training materials  
- Media style guides  
- Diversity and inclusion standards  
- Professional communication protocols

NOTES:  
  
Political correctness often emerges through evolving cultural norms, institutional policies, social expectations, and changing definitions regarding acceptable language. Different groups may disagree about its purpose, effectiveness, scope, or consequences.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Language standards can influence perception by affecting which terms become socially acceptable, discouraged, promoted, or stigmatized. Changes in acceptable terminology often precede broader shifts in social interpretation and collective understanding.
