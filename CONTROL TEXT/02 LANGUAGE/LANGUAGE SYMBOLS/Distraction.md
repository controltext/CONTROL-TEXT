SYSTEM [[09 ATTENTION EXTRACTION]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The diversion of attention away from a current focus, objective, task, thought, or subject toward an alternative stimulus, signal, activity, or source of information.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Redirects cognitive resources by interrupting concentration and shifting awareness toward competing inputs or points of interest.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media interruptions  
- Smartphone notifications  
- Breaking news alerts  
- Pop-up advertisements  
- Background conversations  
- Entertainment media  
- Multitasking environments

NOTES:  
  
Distractions may arise internally or externally and can be intentional or unintentional. Their effectiveness often depends on novelty, emotional significance, urgency, personal relevance, or perceived importance.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention follows salience. Distraction occurs when a competing stimulus successfully presents itself as more relevant, urgent, rewarding, novel, or important than the current focus of awareness.
