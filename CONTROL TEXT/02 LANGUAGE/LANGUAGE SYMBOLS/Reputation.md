SYSTEM [[12 IDENTITY PROGRAMMING]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The collective perception, evaluation, or judgment of an individual, group, organization, or entity based on observed actions, communicated information, experiences, and social interpretation.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Shapes identity by influencing how individuals and groups are recognized, trusted, valued, categorized, and remembered within a social environment.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Personal reputation  
- Professional reputation  
- Brand reputation  
- Community standing  
- Public image  
- Institutional reputation  
- Social status

NOTES:  
  
Reputation develops through repeated interactions, observations, narratives, experiences, and social communication. It may be influenced by direct experience, indirect information, public perception, or collective judgment.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Identity exists not only in self-perception but also in collective perception. Reputation functions as a social identity layer through which individuals and groups are interpreted, evaluated, and understood by others.
