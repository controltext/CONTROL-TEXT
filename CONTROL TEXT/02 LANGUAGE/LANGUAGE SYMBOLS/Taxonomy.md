DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured system for organizing, classifying, naming, and arranging entities, concepts, objects, phenomena, or categories according to defined relationships and hierarchies.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides a framework through which classifications can be systematically organized, communicated, navigated, and understood.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Biological taxonomy  
- Information hierarchies  
- Classification systems  
- Library cataloging systems  
- Product categorization systems

NOTES:  
  
Taxonomies establish relationships between categories and define how entities are grouped, differentiated, and organized within larger classification systems. Different taxonomies may produce different understandings of the same reality depending on their structure and criteria.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Classification creates categories. Taxonomy organizes categories. By arranging classifications into structured hierarchies, taxonomies provide the framework through which names, labels, identities, and meanings can be systematically assigned and navigated.
