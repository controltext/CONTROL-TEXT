SYSTEM [[16 THE SPELL FACTORY]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A statement, authorization, approval, or declaration that grants the right, ability, legitimacy, or allowance to perform an action, access a resource, assume a role, or engage in a behavior.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Uses language to remove restrictions and establish recognized authorization for action within a social, legal, institutional, or symbolic framework.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Parental permission  
- Legal authorization  
- Security clearance  
- Access approval  
- Official consent  
- Operational authorization  
- Verbal approval

NOTES:  
  
Permissions often derive their influence from recognized authority, social agreement, institutional structure, legal systems, cultural norms, or collective acceptance. Permission may be explicit or implicit, temporary or permanent, broad or limited.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Some forms of language do not direct action but authorize it. Permission functions as a performative speech act that changes what is considered acceptable, legitimate, or allowed within a system. By granting authorization through words, permission alters the range of actions available to individuals and groups.
