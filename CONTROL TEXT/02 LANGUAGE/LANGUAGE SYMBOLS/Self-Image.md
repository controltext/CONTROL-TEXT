SYSTEM [[12 IDENTITY PROGRAMMING]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual's perception, understanding, evaluation, and mental representation of who they are.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides an internal identity framework through which individuals interpret their characteristics, abilities, roles, value, appearance, behavior, and place within the world.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Personal self-concept  
- Self-descriptions  
- Internal identity narratives  
- Personal strengths and weaknesses  
- Perceived social status  
- Self-esteem frameworks  
- Personal identity beliefs

NOTES:  
  
Self-image develops through personal experiences, social interactions, feedback, beliefs, memories, achievements, failures, and cultural influences. It may align with or differ from how others perceive an individual.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Identity exists both internally and externally. While reputation reflects how others perceive an individual, self-image reflects how an individual perceives themselves. The relationship between these two forms of identity often influences behavior, confidence, belonging, and personal development.
