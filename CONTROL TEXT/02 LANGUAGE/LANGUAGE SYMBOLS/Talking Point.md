SYSTEM [[11 MEMETIC ENGINEERING]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A concise statement, argument, phrase, or message designed to communicate, reinforce, and replicate a specific idea, narrative, position, or interpretation.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Facilitates the consistent transmission of ideas by providing individuals with easily repeatable language that can be distributed across conversations, media, institutions, and social networks.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political messaging  
- Campaign statements  
- Media soundbites  
- Organizational messaging  
- Public relations statements  
- Advocacy arguments  
- Corporate communications

NOTES:  
  
Talking points simplify complex ideas into repeatable forms that can be consistently communicated by multiple individuals. Their effectiveness often depends upon clarity, repetition, memorability, and alignment with existing narratives.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Ideas spread more easily when they can be repeated without modification. Talking points function as memetic transmission units, allowing narratives, beliefs, and interpretations to propagate through large populations while maintaining message consistency.
