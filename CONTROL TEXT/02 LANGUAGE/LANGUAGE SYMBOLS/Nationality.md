DEFINITION:  
  
An identity classification based on membership, citizenship, affiliation, or association with a nation, state, or national community.

Provides a collective identity framework through which individuals are associated with shared histories, cultures, institutions, territories, symbols, and social groups.

NOTES:  
  
Nationality often serves as a large-scale identity category that connects individuals to collective histories, traditions, institutions, symbols, laws, and cultural narratives. It may be acquired through citizenship, birth, ancestry, or social affiliation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Nationality transforms membership in a political or cultural collective into a personal identity marker. Through shared symbols, narratives, histories, and institutions, individuals may come to understand themselves as participants in a larger national identity.
