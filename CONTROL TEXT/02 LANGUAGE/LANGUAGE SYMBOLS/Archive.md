NOTES:  
  
Archives preserve information that may otherwise be lost to time. They function as repositories of memory, allowing future generations to access records, texts, knowledge, narratives, and historical materials long after their original creation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information gains persistence when it is preserved. Archives transform temporary documents into durable records, allowing knowledge, memory, authority, identity, and history to survive beyond the moment of their creation and remain available for future interpretation.
