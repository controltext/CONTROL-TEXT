SYSTEM [[07 SEMANTIC WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The strategic use of language to influence meaning, interpretation, perception, and understanding by shaping how words, symbols, and concepts are defined and understood.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Influences thought and behavior by controlling the linguistic frameworks through which reality is interpreted.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political messaging  
- Corporate branding  
- Media framing  
- Terminology disputes  
- Ideological language shifts  
- Public relations campaigns  
- Information campaigns

NOTES:  
  
Semantic warfare operates through the control of definitions, labels, associations, and linguistic frameworks. Competing groups often struggle not only over facts and events but over the language used to describe them.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Control over language often becomes control over interpretation. When the meaning of words changes, the perceived meaning of reality may change as well. Conflicts over language are frequently conflicts over perception, identity, authority, and belief.
