DEFINITION:  
  
A deliberately created symbol designed to represent, encode, concentrate, or communicate a specific idea, intention, identity, belief, purpose, or meaning.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Condenses abstract concepts into symbolic form, allowing complex meanings, intentions, identities, or associations to be represented through a single visual symbol.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Magical sigils  
- Occult symbols  
- Personal sigils  
- Organizational seals  
- Heraldic marks  
- Esoteric diagrams  
- Custom symbolic designs

NOTES:  
  
Sigils are often created through deliberate symbolic construction rather than emerging through tradition alone. Their significance is derived from the meanings, intentions, identities, beliefs, or narratives attached to them.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
A sigil functions as a symbolic compression mechanism. Complex ideas, intentions, identities, or meanings can be condensed into a single image that serves as a focal point for attention, memory, interpretation, and symbolic association.
