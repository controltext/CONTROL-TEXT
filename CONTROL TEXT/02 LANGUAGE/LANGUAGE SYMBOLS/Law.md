DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A formally established rule, principle, or standard recognized and enforced by a governing authority within a society, institution, or community.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Creates and maintains social order by defining acceptable behavior, resolving disputes, establishing expectations, and reinforcing collective standards.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Criminal law  
- Civil law  
- Constitutional law  
- Corporate regulations  
- Local ordinances  
- Professional standards  
- Institutional policies

NOTES:  
  
Laws often emerge from historical traditions, social norms, political processes, cultural values, and collective agreements. While laws may change over time, they function as formal expressions of what a society considers permissible, prohibited, or required.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Law transforms collective expectations into institutional structures. While social norms influence behavior through informal pressure, laws reinforce behavior through formal recognition, authority, and enforcement.
