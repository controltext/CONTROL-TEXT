DEFINITION:  
  
A foundational document that establishes the core principles, structure, authority, rights, responsibilities, and governing framework of a group, institution, organization, or society.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides an authoritative reference through which legitimacy, order, identity, rules, and collective expectations are defined, preserved, and communicated.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- National constitutions  
- Organizational charters  
- Founding documents  
- Institutional constitutions  
- Governance frameworks  
- Legal foundations  
- Foundational agreements

NOTES:  
  
Constitutions often serve as foundational texts that define the principles upon which systems of governance, authority, identity, and collective organization are built. They may be interpreted, amended, preserved, and referenced across generations.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Sacred texts provide continuity by preserving foundational principles across time. A constitution functions as an authoritative source of legitimacy and meaning, allowing institutions and societies to maintain continuity by referencing a common foundational framework.
