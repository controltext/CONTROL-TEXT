SYSTEM [[10 CONSENSUS REALITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A shared expectation, standard, or pattern of behavior that is widely accepted, practiced, or reinforced within a group, community, culture, or society.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Guides behavior by establishing what is considered normal, acceptable, appropriate, or expected within a collective environment.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social etiquette  
- Cultural customs  
- Dress expectations  
- Workplace behavior  
- Communication standards  
- Public conduct  
- Community traditions

NOTES:  
  
Social norms may be explicit or implicit, formal or informal. They are often maintained through approval, disapproval, imitation, reinforcement, expectation, and social feedback rather than formal authority.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
People frequently adjust behavior based on perceived expectations from others. Social norms create a shared behavioral framework that helps stabilize collective reality by establishing what is considered ordinary, acceptable, or legitimate.
