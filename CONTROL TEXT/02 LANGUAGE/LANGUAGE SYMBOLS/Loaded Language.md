SYSTEM [[07 SEMANTIC WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The use of words, phrases, labels, or descriptions that carry strong emotional, cultural, moral, or ideological associations intended to influence perception and response.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Shapes interpretation by triggering emotional reactions, biases, values, or assumptions before analytical evaluation occurs.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- "Freedom Fighter" vs "Terrorist"  
- "Patriot" vs "Extremist"  
- "Victim" vs "Survivor"  
- "Conspiracy Theory"  
- "Enemy of the People"  
- "Corporate Greed"  
- "National Security Threat"

NOTES:  
  
Loaded language can influence perception by embedding emotional or moral judgments directly into terminology. The choice of words often affects interpretation before facts or arguments are considered.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Words do more than communicate information. They can signal approval, disapproval, danger, legitimacy, trust, fear, or belonging. Language often shapes emotional response before conscious analysis begins.
