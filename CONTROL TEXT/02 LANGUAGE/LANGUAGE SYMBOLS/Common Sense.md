SYSTEM [[10 CONSENSUS REALITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A collection of beliefs, assumptions, interpretations, and judgments that are widely perceived as obvious, practical, reasonable, or self-evident within a particular group, culture, or society.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides a shared framework for interpreting reality by establishing assumptions that require little explanation, justification, or examination.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Everyday assumptions  
- Practical wisdom  
- Conventional understandings  
- Social expectations  
- Common knowledge  
- Cultural assumptions  
- Widely accepted explanations

NOTES:  
  
Common sense varies across cultures, time periods, communities, and social environments. Ideas considered obvious in one context may appear unfamiliar, irrational, or controversial in another.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
The strongest forms of consensus often become invisible. When an assumption is widely accepted as common sense, it may no longer be perceived as a belief at all, but as an unquestioned feature of reality itself.
