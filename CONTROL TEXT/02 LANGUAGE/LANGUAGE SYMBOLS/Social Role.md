SYSTEM [[12 IDENTITY PROGRAMMING]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A recognized position, function, or pattern of behavior associated with an individual's place within a group, relationship, institution, or society.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides a framework for identity by defining expected behaviors, responsibilities, relationships, and forms of participation within a social environment.

- Parent

- Leader

NOTES:  
  
Social roles help organize interactions by establishing expectations regarding behavior, responsibility, authority, cooperation, and participation. Individuals often occupy multiple social roles simultaneously across different contexts.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Identity is frequently shaped through participation in social structures. Social roles provide ready-made frameworks through which individuals understand themselves, relate to others, and navigate collective environments.
