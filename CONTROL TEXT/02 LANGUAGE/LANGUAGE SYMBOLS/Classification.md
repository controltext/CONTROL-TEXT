SYSTEMS [[15 THE NAME MACHINE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process of organizing, categorizing, grouping, or assigning entities, concepts, objects, people, events, or ideas into defined classes, labels, categories, or types based upon selected characteristics or criteria.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Creates order and meaning by transforming complexity into recognizable categories that can be named, communicated, compared, understood, and managed.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Scientific taxonomy  
- Social categories  
- Product categories  
- Personality classifications  
- Political classifications  
- Educational classifications  
- Information categorization systems

NOTES:  
  
Classification systems allow individuals and institutions to organize information, identities, objects, and experiences into structured frameworks. Different classification systems may produce different interpretations of the same phenomena depending upon the criteria selected.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Before something can be discussed, managed, studied, remembered, or transmitted, it is often classified. Classification transforms continuous reality into discrete categories, creating the structures through which names, identities, meanings, and relationships are assigned and communicated.
