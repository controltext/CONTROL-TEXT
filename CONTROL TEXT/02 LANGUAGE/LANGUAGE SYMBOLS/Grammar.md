SYSTEM [[17 LANGUAGE ENGINE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A system of rules, conventions, and structures that governs how words, symbols, phrases, and sentences are organized and interpreted within a language.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides the framework through which linguistic elements can be combined into coherent, understandable, and communicable forms.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Sentence structure  
- Verb conjugation  
- Word order  
- Punctuation rules  
- Syntactic conventions  
- Linguistic agreements  
- Language construction rules

NOTES:  
  
Grammar establishes the structural rules that allow language users to communicate complex ideas consistently. Different languages may employ different grammatical systems while serving similar communicative functions.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Words alone do not create meaning. Grammar functions as the organizational engine that determines how linguistic components interact, transforming isolated symbols into structured expressions capable of conveying complex information, relationships, intentions, and ideas.
