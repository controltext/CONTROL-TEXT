SYSTEM [[07 SEMANTIC WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process of changing the language, symbols, identity, presentation, or associations connected to a person, organization, product, institution, or idea in order to influence perception.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Alters interpretation by replacing existing meanings, associations, or narratives with new ones designed to shape public understanding and response.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Corporate rebranding campaigns  
- Political image reconstruction  
- Product renaming  
- Institutional renaming  
- Public relations campaigns  
- Reputation management efforts  
- Historical revision of identities

NOTES:  
  
Rebranding seeks to modify how a subject is perceived by changing the symbolic framework surrounding it. While the underlying reality may remain unchanged, the language, imagery, and associations connected to it can be transformed.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Perception is often influenced as much by presentation as by substance. Changing a name, label, or identity can alter interpretation even when underlying structures remain largely the same.
