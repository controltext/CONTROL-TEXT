SYSTEM [[10 CONSENSUS REALITY]]
DOMAIN [[THE LANGUAGE]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A formally recognized account of past events that is preserved, taught, communicated, and reinforced by institutions, authorities, educational systems, or cultural structures.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides a shared interpretation of the past that helps establish collective memory, identity, legitimacy, continuity, and social understanding.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- School history curricula  
- National histories  
- Government archives  
- Historical textbooks  
- Museum narratives  
- Public commemorations  
- Institutional records

NOTES:  
  
Official histories are often constructed through the selection, preservation, interpretation, and communication of historical events. Different societies, institutions, and groups may maintain different official histories regarding the same events.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Collective understanding of the past is frequently shaped through institutional narratives. Official history functions as a mechanism for organizing memory, reinforcing identity, and establishing a shared framework through which a group interprets its origins, development, and place in reality.
