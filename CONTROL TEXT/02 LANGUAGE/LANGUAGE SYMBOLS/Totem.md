DEFINITION:  
  
A symbol, object, image, animal, figure, or representation that embodies the identity, values, beliefs, history, power, or collective spirit of an individual or group.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Acts as a focal point for symbolic meaning by concentrating identity, loyalty, memory, and emotional attachment into a recognizable form.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Tribal totems  
- National symbols  
- Mascots  
- Religious icons  
- Sacred animals  
- Corporate mascots

NOTES:  
  
Totems serve as symbolic anchors for collective identity. Their influence emerges not from the object itself, but from the meanings, stories, values, and emotions associated with them.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
A totem transforms abstract identity into visible form. Through repeated association, groups often project loyalty, belonging, memory, purpose, and meaning onto the symbol, allowing it to function as a symbolic embodiment of the collective.
