DEFINITION:  
  
A formal document that records, defines, and preserves agreements, obligations, responsibilities, rights, expectations, or commitments between individuals, groups, organizations, or entities.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Creates a durable textual framework through which agreements can be communicated, referenced, enforced, interpreted, and maintained across time.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Employment contracts  
- Business agreements  
- Service agreements  
- Lease agreements  
- Partnership agreements  
- Licensing agreements  
- Commercial contracts

NOTES:  
  
Contracts formalize expectations by converting verbal or informal understandings into documented agreements. Their authority often derives from mutual recognition, institutional support, legal systems, and written preservation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Agreements become more durable when they are recorded. Contracts transform intentions, promises, obligations, and expectations into textual artifacts that can persist beyond memory, allowing cooperation and coordination to occur across time, distance, and changing circumstances.
