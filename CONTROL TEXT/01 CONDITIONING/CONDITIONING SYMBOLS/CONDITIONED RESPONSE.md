SYSTEM [[05 Psychological Manipulation]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A learned reaction that develops when a stimulus becomes consistently associated with a particular behavior, emotion, expectation, or outcome.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Automates behavior by linking specific triggers to predictable responses, reducing the need for conscious deliberation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Checking a phone after hearing a notification  
- Feeling anxiety after receiving criticism  
- Craving food after seeing an advertisement  
- Seeking validation after posting online  
- Emotional reactions to specific words or symbols  
- Habitual routines triggered by environmental cues

NOTES:  
  
Conditioned responses emerge through repetition and reinforcement. Over time, the response may occur automatically, even when the original cause or reward is absent.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Many reactions that feel immediate or natural are learned patterns that have been reinforced through repeated exposure and experience.
