SYSTEM [[06 Narrative Control]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
Narratives that emphasize differences, conflicts, oppositions, or competing identities in ways that separate individuals or groups into distinct sides, categories, or camps.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Creates social, political, cultural, or ideological division by reinforcing distinctions between "us" and "them."  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Partisan political conflicts  
- Culture wars  
- Class conflict narratives  
- Religious divisions  
- National rivalries  
- Generational conflicts  
- Identity-based polarization

NOTES:  
  
Divisive storylines often simplify complex situations into opposing camps, making it easier to identify allies, opponents, heroes, and villains. While divisions may reflect real disagreements, narratives can amplify, reduce, or redefine those differences.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Division often strengthens group identity. The clearer the distinction between "us" and "them," the easier it becomes to mobilize loyalty, participation, and emotional investment.
