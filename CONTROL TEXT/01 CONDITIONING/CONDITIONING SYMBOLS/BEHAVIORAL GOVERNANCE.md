SYSTEM [[04 Power Corruption]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The shaping, directing, regulating, and influencing of individual or collective behavior through rules, incentives, penalties, norms, and institutional structures.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Aligns behavior with the objectives of a system by rewarding compliance, discouraging deviation, and establishing acceptable patterns of conduct.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Laws and regulations  
- Workplace policies  
- School disciplinary systems  
- Social norms  
- Community guidelines  
- Reward programs  
- Penalty structures

NOTES:  
  
Behavior governance does not always rely on direct force. Systems often shape behavior through incentives, expectations, habits, social pressure, access control, and perceived consequences.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
The most effective forms of governance often become invisible. When rules are internalized, individuals may regulate themselves without requiring constant external enforcement.
