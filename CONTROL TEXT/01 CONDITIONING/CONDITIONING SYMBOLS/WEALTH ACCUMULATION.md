SYSTEM [[03 Economic Control]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

  
DEFINITION:  
  
The process of acquiring, retaining, and increasing financial resources, assets, capital, or economic influence over time.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Concentrates resources by preserving and expanding value through ownership, investment, inheritance, production, or exchange.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Savings accounts  
- Real estate ownership  
- Investment portfolios  
- Business ownership  
- Capital gains  
- Generational wealth  
- Asset appreciation

NOTES:  
  
Wealth accumulation allows resources to persist across time and can increase an individual's or organization's capacity to influence future outcomes. Accumulated wealth often generates opportunities unavailable to those without comparable resources.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Wealth functions as stored optionality. The greater the accumulation of resources, the greater the ability to absorb risk, pursue opportunities, and influence surrounding systems.
