SYSTEM [[04 Power Corruption]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process through which authority, influence, decision-making capacity, or control become concentrated within a smaller number of individuals, organizations, or institutions.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Reduces the distribution of power by centralizing control over resources, information, policies, or systems.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Corporate monopolies  
- Political centralization  
- Executive authority expansion  
- Institutional mergers  
- Media ownership concentration  
- Control of critical infrastructure  
- Platform dominance

NOTES:  
  
Power consolidation can occur through accumulation, acquisition, inheritance, regulation, technological control, or institutional design. As power becomes concentrated, fewer actors gain greater influence over system outcomes.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Power often seeks stability. One method of achieving stability is reducing the number of entities capable of influencing important decisions.
