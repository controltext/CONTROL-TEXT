SYSTEM [[03 Economic Control]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process through which worth, importance, utility, or desirability is attributed to a resource, object, service, idea, person, or activity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Determines what receives attention, investment, protection, compensation, and pursuit within a system.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Market pricing  
- Salary determination  
- Brand valuation  
- Real estate appraisal  
- Academic credentials  
- Social status  
- Scarcity-based pricing

NOTES:  
  
Value assignment is not limited to money. Societies assign value to ideas, identities, skills, resources, institutions, and behaviors. Different systems often assign value according to different criteria.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Value is often perceived as inherent, yet many forms of value emerge from collective agreement, cultural beliefs, scarcity, utility, or system design.
