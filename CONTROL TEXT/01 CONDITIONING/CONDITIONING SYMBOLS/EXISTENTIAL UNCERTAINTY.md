SYSTEM [[02 Existential Deconstruction]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The recognition that fundamental aspects of existence, meaning, purpose, identity, and the future cannot be known with complete certainty.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Introduces ambiguity into human experience by preventing absolute certainty about reality, purpose, and ultimate outcomes.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Questioning life's purpose  
- Fear of the unknown  
- Uncertainty about the future  
- Spiritual doubt  
- Identity confusion  
- Mortality awareness  
- Searching for meaning

NOTES:  
  
Existential uncertainty emerges when individuals recognize the limits of knowledge, prediction, and certainty. It often motivates the creation of belief systems, identities, narratives, and structures that provide orientation and meaning.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Many systems of meaning, identity, and authority arise as responses to existential uncertainty rather than solutions to it.
