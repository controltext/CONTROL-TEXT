SYSTEM [[02 Existential Deconstruction]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The limitations, boundaries, and conditions that cannot be completely avoided, transcended, or eliminated within existence.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Establishes the framework within which all choices, actions, and experiences occur by defining the limits of possibility.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Mortality  
- Physical limitations  
- Time constraints  
- Resource scarcity  
- Biological needs  
- Environmental conditions  
- Uncertainty

NOTES:  
  
Inescapable constraints exist regardless of belief, preference, ideology, or intention. While constraints can sometimes be modified, exchanged, or temporarily overcome, they cannot be entirely removed from existence.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Freedom often exists not in the absence of constraints, but in how individuals navigate the constraints they cannot avoid.
