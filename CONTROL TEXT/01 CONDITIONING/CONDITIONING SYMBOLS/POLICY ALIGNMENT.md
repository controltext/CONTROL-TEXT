DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process by which policies, regulations, procedures, and institutional rules are designed or adjusted to support specific interests, objectives, or power structures.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Directs behavior by establishing incentives, restrictions, permissions, and consequences that encourage alignment with institutional goals.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Corporate lobbying  
- Regulatory frameworks  
- Workplace policies  
- Government legislation  
- Platform community guidelines  
- Tax incentives  
- Licensing requirements

NOTES:  
  
Policy alignment can emerge through deliberate design, institutional inertia, or the influence of individuals and groups with decision-making authority. Policies often shape outcomes indirectly by altering the environment in which decisions are made.
