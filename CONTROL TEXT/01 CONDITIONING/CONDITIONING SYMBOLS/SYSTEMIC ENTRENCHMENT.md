SYSTEM [[04 Power Corruption]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process through which systems, institutions, structures, and power relationships become deeply embedded, resistant to change, and capable of reproducing themselves over time.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Preserves existing arrangements by reinforcing stability, continuity, and resistance to disruption or transformation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Bureaucratic inertia  
- Institutional legacy systems  
- Long-standing political structures  
- Corporate monopolies  
- Cultural traditions  
- Generational wealth systems  
- Regulatory capture

NOTES:  
  
Systemic entrenchment occurs when structures become self-reinforcing. Over time, systems may develop mechanisms that preserve themselves independent of the intentions of any individual participant.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
The most durable systems often persist because they become woven into habits, expectations, institutions, identities, and cultural assumptions. Change becomes difficult not because it is impossible, but because the system continuously reproduces itself.
