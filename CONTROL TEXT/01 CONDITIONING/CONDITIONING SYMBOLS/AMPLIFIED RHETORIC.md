SYSTEM [[06 Narrative Control]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The deliberate intensification of language, imagery, emotion, or messaging to increase attention, persuasion, urgency, or perceived significance.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Enhances the impact of a narrative by making messages appear more important, threatening, inspiring, shocking, or emotionally compelling than they might otherwise seem.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Sensational headlines  
- Political speeches  
- Fear-based messaging  
- Emotional advertising  
- Moral panic narratives  
- Revolutionary slogans  
- Crisis framing

NOTES:  
  
Amplified rhetoric increases engagement by activating emotional, social, or psychological responses. The effectiveness of a message is often influenced as much by its presentation as by its factual content.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
The emotional intensity of a message can influence its perceived importance. Narratives that generate strong emotional reactions often spread more rapidly and persist longer within public consciousness.
