SYSTEM [[06 Narrative Control]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]


DEFINITION:  
  
The process of emphasizing, omitting, organizing, or presenting information in ways that influence interpretation, perception, and understanding.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Shapes meaning by directing attention toward certain aspects of reality while minimizing, excluding, or de-emphasizing others.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- News reporting angles  
- Statistical presentation  
- Historical interpretation  
- Political messaging  
- Advertising campaigns  
- Documentary editing  
- Headline construction

NOTES:  
  
Selective framing does not necessarily require false information. Meaning can be influenced through the selection, arrangement, emphasis, timing, or omission of otherwise accurate information.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
The way information is presented often influences perception as much as the information itself. Different frames can produce radically different interpretations of the same event.
