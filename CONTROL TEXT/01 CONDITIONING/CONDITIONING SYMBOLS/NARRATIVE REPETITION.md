SYSTEM [[06 Narrative Control]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The repeated presentation, reinforcement, and circulation of a narrative, message, theme, or interpretation across time, media, and social environments.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Increases familiarity, memorability, and perceived legitimacy by exposing individuals to the same narrative repeatedly.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political slogans  
- Advertising campaigns  
- News cycles  
- Brand messaging  
- Historical narratives  
- Cultural myths  
- Social media talking points

NOTES:  
  
Narrative repetition increases familiarity regardless of whether a narrative is accurate, inaccurate, beneficial, or harmful. Repeated exposure can strengthen recall, recognition, and perceived normality.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Familiarity often becomes confused with truth. Repetition can transform an unfamiliar idea into a familiar assumption, and a familiar assumption into a perceived reality.
