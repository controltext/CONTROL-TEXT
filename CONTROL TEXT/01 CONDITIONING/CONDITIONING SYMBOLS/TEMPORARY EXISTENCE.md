SYSTEM [[02 Existential Deconstruction]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The condition that all forms, experiences, identities, structures, and living beings exist only for a finite period before changing, dissolving, or ending.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Establishes impermanence as a fundamental characteristic of existence, preventing permanence and fixed continuity.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Human life  
- Civilizations  
- Relationships  
- Institutions  
- Empires  
- Technologies  
- Cultural movements

NOTES:  
  
Temporary existence applies equally to individuals, organizations, ideas, and systems. What appears permanent often persists only because its rate of change is difficult to perceive.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Many human systems seek permanence despite existing within conditions of impermanence. The tension between permanence and impermanence drives much of human behavior.
