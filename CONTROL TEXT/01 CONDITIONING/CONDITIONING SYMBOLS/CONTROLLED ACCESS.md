SYSTEM [[04 Power Corruption]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The regulation of entry, participation, resources, information, opportunities, or privileges through systems of permission, restriction, qualification, or exclusion.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Determines who can obtain access to valuable resources, positions, knowledge, networks, or decision-making processes.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Membership requirements  
- Security clearances  
- Paywalls  
- Licensing systems  
- Exclusive organizations  
- Admission standards  
- Invitation-only networks

NOTES:  
  
Controlled access influences outcomes by determining who may participate, contribute, observe, or benefit from a system. Access can be restricted through economic, social, institutional, technological, or informational barriers.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Power often expresses itself through gatekeeping. The ability to grant, deny, or condition access can be as influential as direct authority.
