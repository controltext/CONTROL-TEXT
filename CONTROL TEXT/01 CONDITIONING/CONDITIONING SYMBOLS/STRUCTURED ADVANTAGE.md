SYSTEM [[04 Power Corruption]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The creation or maintenance of systems, rules, institutions, or conditions that consistently benefit certain individuals, groups, or organizations over others.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Produces unequal outcomes by embedding advantages into the structure of a system rather than relying solely on individual actions.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Legacy admissions  
- Regulatory capture  
- Exclusive networks  
- Preferential access  
- Institutional favoritism  
- Information asymmetry  
- Monopoly protections

NOTES:  
  
Structured advantage often persists beyond the individuals who created it. Once embedded into institutions, procedures, or cultural norms, advantages can reproduce themselves across time.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Systems do not need to explicitly declare favoritism to produce unequal outcomes. Advantages can emerge through rules, incentives, access, timing, or accumulated position within a structure.
