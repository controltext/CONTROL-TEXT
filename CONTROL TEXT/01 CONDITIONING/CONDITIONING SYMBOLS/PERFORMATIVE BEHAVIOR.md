SYSTEM [[05 Psychological Manipulation]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
Behavior performed primarily to communicate identity, status, belief, affiliation, virtue, competence, or social alignment to others rather than solely to achieve a practical outcome.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms internal beliefs, identities, and social expectations into visible actions that signal membership, values, or position within a group or system.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Virtue signaling  
- Brand loyalty displays  
- Political expression  
- Social media posting  
- Status consumption  
- Public declarations  
- Group conformity rituals

NOTES:  
  
Performative behavior is not inherently deceptive. Individuals often engage in performative actions to communicate identity, belonging, values, competence, or social position. The behavior becomes performative when the act itself functions as a signal to an audience.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
People frequently perform versions of themselves in response to perceived expectations, social incentives, and audience feedback. Behavior can become a form of communication as much as a means of achieving practical outcomes.
