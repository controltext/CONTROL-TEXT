SYSTEM [[06 Narrative Control]]
DOMAIN [[THE CONDITIONING]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The preservation of uncertainty, ambiguity, or lack of direct evidence in a manner that allows responsibility, intent, knowledge, or involvement to be denied.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Protects individuals, groups, institutions, or narratives from direct accountability by maintaining multiple interpretations or insufficient proof.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Ambiguous statements  
- Indirect communication  
- Unofficial actions  
- Information compartmentalization  
- Anonymous sourcing  
- Strategic vagueness  
- Unverifiable claims

NOTES:  
  
Plausible deniability does not require innocence or guilt. It functions by preserving uncertainty regarding responsibility, intent, authorship, or causation. The absence of certainty becomes a protective mechanism.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Narratives often gain resilience when responsibility cannot be clearly assigned. Ambiguity can function as a form of strategic protection, allowing multiple interpretations to coexist while limiting direct accountability.
