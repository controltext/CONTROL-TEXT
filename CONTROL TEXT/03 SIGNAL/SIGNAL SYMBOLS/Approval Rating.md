SYSTEM [[19 MANUFACTURED CONSENT]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A statistical measure that expresses the percentage of a population reporting approval, support, confidence, satisfaction, or favorable opinion toward a person, institution, policy, organization, decision, or leadership entity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms individual opinions into a visible metric that communicates perceived legitimacy, popularity, trust, confidence, or public acceptance.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Presidential approval ratings  
- Government approval ratings  
- Corporate leadership ratings  
- Institutional confidence ratings  
- Policy approval ratings  
- Public satisfaction measurements  
- Leadership support metrics

NOTES:  
  
Approval ratings are typically derived from polling and survey data. Their interpretation may be influenced by methodology, sample selection, question wording, timing, media coverage, public events, and presentation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Numerical representations of opinion often carry persuasive power beyond the opinions themselves. Approval ratings function as legitimacy indicators that transform distributed public sentiment into visible social metrics, influencing perception by signaling levels of support, confidence, popularity, trust, or acceptance within a population.
