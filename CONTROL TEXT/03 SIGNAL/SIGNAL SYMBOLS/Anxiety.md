SYSTEM [[23 EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An emotional state characterized by apprehension, worry, unease, tension, vigilance, anticipation, or concern regarding uncertain, ambiguous, unpredictable, or potentially threatening future events or conditions.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Directs attention toward uncertainty and potential risk, increasing monitoring, anticipation, preparation, vigilance, and future-oriented evaluation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social anxiety  
- Performance anxiety  
- Financial anxiety  
- Health anxiety  
- Existential anxiety  
- Relationship anxiety  
- Future uncertainty

NOTES:  
  
Anxiety differs from fear. Fear is typically directed toward a specific and identifiable threat, while anxiety is often directed toward uncertain, future, ambiguous, or undefined possibilities. Anxiety may motivate preparation, avoidance, vigilance, planning, or rumination.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
The unknown often exerts emotional influence before any event occurs. Anxiety functions as an uncertainty-amplification mechanism that transforms ambiguity into heightened attention and anticipation. By directing awareness toward possible future threats, risks, or failures, anxiety influences planning, decision-making, interpretation, behavior, and perception long before outcomes become known.
