SYSTEM [[24 SURVEILLANCE SOCIETY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A chronological record of actions, events, transactions, decisions, modifications, communications, accesses, or activities that documents what occurred, when it occurred, and often who was involved.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms activities into traceable histories by preserving evidence of actions across time, allowing reconstruction, verification, investigation, accountability, analysis, and review.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Login histories  
- Financial transaction logs  
- Access records  
- System change logs  
- Communication records  
- Document revision histories  
- Activity logs

NOTES:  
  
Audit trails may be generated automatically or manually. Their significance depends upon completeness, accuracy, integrity, retention policies, accessibility, and the ability to associate events with identities, systems, or actions.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Observation records events. Audit trails record sequences of responsibility. An audit trail functions as an accountability-preservation mechanism that transforms actions into permanent histories. Through audit trails, past behavior becomes reconstructable, decisions become reviewable, and activities become traceable across time, enabling verification, investigation, governance, and institutional memory.
