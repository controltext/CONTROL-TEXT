SYSTEM [[24 SURVEILLANCE SOCIETY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A biometric identification and recognition process that analyzes facial features, structures, proportions, patterns, or visual characteristics in order to identify, verify, classify, compare, or track individuals.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms visual observations of faces into identity-linked information, enabling recognition, verification, tracking, classification, and automated identification across locations and time.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Smartphone face authentication  
- Security access systems  
- Airport identification systems  
- Surveillance camera matching  
- Missing person identification  
- Photo tagging systems  
- Automated identity verification

NOTES:  
  
Facial recognition systems depend upon image quality, biometric models, comparison databases, environmental conditions, and matching thresholds. Different systems may vary significantly in accuracy, scope, and application.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Observation becomes identity-linked when recognition is automated. Facial recognition functions as a visual identity-resolution mechanism that connects faces to records, profiles, histories, and databases. Through facial recognition, anonymous observation can become identifiable observation, allowing individuals to be recognized across locations, systems, encounters, and time.
