SYSTEM [[20 ALGORITHMIC GOVERNANCE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The execution of actions, processes, decisions, responses, workflows, or operations by systems according to predefined rules, logic, algorithms, models, or procedures with limited or no direct human intervention.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transfers operational control from continuous human decision-making to repeatable system-driven processes that can execute tasks consistently, rapidly, and at scale.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Automated recommendations  
- Automated moderation  
- Automated trading  
- Automated scheduling  
- Automated manufacturing  
- Automated customer service  
- Automated decision systems

NOTES:  
  
Automation may operate through simple rules, complex algorithms, machine learning systems, statistical models, workflow engines, or hybrid human-machine processes. Its influence often depends upon the goals, assumptions, constraints, and objectives encoded into the system.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Governance changes when decisions become processes. Automation functions as an execution mechanism that transforms rules, rankings, recommendations, and optimizations into continuous operational behavior. By embedding decision logic into systems, automation enables influence, management, coordination, and control to occur at scales beyond direct human supervision.
