SYSTEMS [[22 PREDICTIVE PROGRAMMING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured description, simulation, model, narrative, or representation of a possible future situation, sequence of events, outcome, condition, or state of affairs.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides a framework for exploring, anticipating, interpreting, planning for, or responding to potential future developments before they occur.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Best-case scenarios  
- Worst-case scenarios  
- Strategic planning scenarios  
- Risk scenarios  
- Crisis simulations  
- Future world models  
- Alternative outcome projections

NOTES:  
  
Scenarios are not predictions. They represent possible futures rather than guaranteed futures. Multiple scenarios may exist simultaneously, each based upon different assumptions, variables, decisions, risks, opportunities, or conditions.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
The future is often explored through possibilities rather than certainties. A scenario functions as a future-modeling mechanism that organizes anticipated events into coherent structures, allowing individuals and systems to mentally rehearse outcomes, evaluate risks, imagine opportunities, and prepare for multiple potential futures before they materialize.
