DEFINITION:  
  
The strategic management of communication, messaging, narratives, relationships, and public perception for the purpose of influencing reputation, trust, legitimacy, understanding, or public opinion.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Shapes how individuals, organizations, institutions, movements, products, or ideas are perceived by influencing narratives, interpretations, attention, and public understanding.

NOTES:  
  
Public relations may involve communication strategies intended to inform, persuade, explain, reassure, clarify, promote, defend, or manage public perception. Its effectiveness often depends upon credibility, consistency, timing, media exposure, narrative alignment, and audience trust.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Perception often influences reality as much as direct experience. Public relations functions as a perception-management system that attempts to shape how people interpret individuals, organizations, events, products, and institutions by influencing narratives, trust, reputation, and public understanding through strategic communication.
