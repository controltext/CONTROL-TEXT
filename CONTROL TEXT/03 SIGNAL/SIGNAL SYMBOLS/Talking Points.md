SYSTEM [[19 MANUFACTURED CONSENT]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A set of prepared messages, phrases, arguments, themes, explanations, or narrative elements intended to guide communication and ensure consistency across speakers, representatives, organizations, or communication channels.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Standardizes messaging by providing communicators with shared language, framing, terminology, and narrative structures that reinforce a desired interpretation or position.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political talking points  
- Corporate messaging guides  
- Media briefing materials  
- Campaign message guides  
- Public relations communications  
- Organizational communication templates  
- Advocacy messaging frameworks

NOTES:  
  
Talking points may be used to improve clarity, consistency, coordination, efficiency, persuasion, or message discipline. Their effectiveness often derives from repetition, alignment, familiarity, and coordinated distribution across communication networks.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Messages gain influence when they are repeated consistently across multiple sources. Talking points function as message-standardization mechanisms that synchronize communication by providing shared language and framing, increasing narrative coherence and reinforcing particular interpretations within an information environment.
