SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A score, evaluation, assessment, grade, or judgment assigned to an individual, group, product, service, organization, behavior, or entity according to selected criteria.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms subjective evaluations into visible signals that communicate perceived quality, trustworthiness, performance, reliability, credibility, satisfaction, or value.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Star ratings  
- Product reviews  
- Seller ratings  
- Driver ratings  
- Restaurant ratings  
- Employee evaluations  
- Service quality ratings

NOTES:  
  
Ratings may be generated by individuals, groups, institutions, algorithms, or hybrid systems. Their influence often depends upon visibility, scale, methodology, participation, weighting, and perceived fairness.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Trust becomes scalable when it is quantified. A rating functions as a reputation signal that converts individual judgments into visible metrics, allowing strangers to evaluate credibility, quality, reliability, and status without direct personal experience. Through ratings, social trust becomes measurable, transferable, and actionable within larger systems.
