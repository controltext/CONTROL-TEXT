SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The level of attention, recognition, approval, support, awareness, engagement, or favorability received by an individual, group, organization, product, idea, or entity within a population or social network.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Acts as a social signal that communicates collective attention and acceptance, influencing visibility, perceived importance, trust, legitimacy, and future engagement.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Popular public figures  
- Trending content  
- Best-selling products  
- Viral media  
- Popular brands  
- High-engagement creators  
- Widely supported ideas

NOTES:  
  
Popularity measures attention and collective interest rather than expertise, truth, authority, quality, or trustworthiness. Popularity and credibility may overlap, but they are not identical.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention often functions as a social resource. Popularity acts as an attention-aggregation mechanism that transforms many individual acts of recognition, engagement, or support into visible social momentum. Through popularity, attention becomes reputation, reputation attracts more attention, and visibility can become self-reinforcing within a network.
