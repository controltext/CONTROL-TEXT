SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A reputation-based scoring or evaluation framework that assigns value, status, trustworthiness, reliability, or standing to individuals or entities based upon observed behaviors, attributes, activities, relationships, evaluations, or compliance with selected criteria.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms behavioral and reputational information into actionable scores that can influence access, opportunities, privileges, restrictions, trust, incentives, or treatment within a system.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Reputation scores  
- Trust scores  
- Community reputation systems  
- User reputation rankings  
- Participation scoring systems  
- Platform trust metrics  
- Behavioral rating systems

NOTES:  
  
Social credit systems may use a wide variety of inputs including ratings, reviews, participation records, behavioral data, compliance metrics, reputation indicators, social relationships, or performance measures. The consequences and criteria vary significantly depending on the governing system.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Reputation becomes governance when it directly affects opportunities and outcomes. A social credit system functions as a reputation-operationalization mechanism that converts evaluations, behaviors, and trust signals into structured scores capable of influencing access, privileges, status, incentives, restrictions, and decision-making within a social environment.
