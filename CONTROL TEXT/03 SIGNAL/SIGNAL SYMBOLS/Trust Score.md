SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A numerical, categorical, or calculated measure intended to represent the perceived trustworthiness, reliability, credibility, dependability, integrity, or confidence associated with an individual, group, organization, system, or entity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms qualitative judgments about trust into measurable signals that can be used to guide decisions, rankings, access, recommendations, risk assessments, and interactions.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Customer trust ratings  
- Seller trust scores  
- Platform trust metrics  
- Reliability indexes  
- Reputation trust rankings  
- Risk-adjusted trust evaluations  
- Community trust indicators

NOTES:  
  
Trust scores may be derived from behavior, history, verification status, ratings, reviews, relationships, transactions, performance metrics, or other indicators. Different systems may calculate trust differently depending on their objectives and evaluation criteria.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Trust is often difficult to assess directly at scale. A trust score functions as a trust-compression mechanism that converts complex histories, behaviors, evaluations, and relationships into simplified indicators of reliability. Through trust scores, systems can rapidly estimate confidence, allocate opportunities, reduce uncertainty, and automate decisions affecting participation, access, and status.
