SYSTEM [[19 MANUFACTURED CONSENT]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured method for collecting, measuring, summarizing, and communicating the opinions, preferences, beliefs, attitudes, intentions, or perceptions of a population or sample group.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms individual responses into collective opinion metrics, allowing public sentiment to be measured, represented, communicated, compared, and interpreted.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political polls  
- Approval ratings  
- Public opinion surveys  
- Market research polls  
- Consumer preference surveys  
- Election polling  
- Social attitude surveys

NOTES:  
  
Polls attempt to estimate the views of larger populations through sampled responses. Their interpretation may depend upon methodology, sampling, question wording, timing, response rates, statistical assumptions, and presentation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Opinions become socially significant when they are aggregated and displayed. Polls function as opinion-mapping mechanisms that transform dispersed individual beliefs into visible collective metrics, influencing perception of public sentiment, social norms, consensus, legitimacy, and majority opinion.
