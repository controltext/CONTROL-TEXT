SYSTEM [[23 EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An emotional state characterized by awareness of another individual's perceived advantage, possession, status, achievement, quality, opportunity, relationship, recognition, or resource, accompanied by a desire for that advantage or discomfort regarding its unequal distribution.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Directs attention toward social comparisons, relative status, perceived inequalities, and desired attributes, influencing motivation, aspiration, resentment, competition, imitation, or self-evaluation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Envy of wealth  
- Envy of status  
- Envy of success  
- Envy of beauty  
- Envy of talent  
- Envy of recognition  
- Envy of opportunity

NOTES:  
  
Envy differs from admiration. Admiration appreciates another's qualities, while envy involves discomfort arising from perceived disparity. Envy may motivate self-improvement, competition, imitation, resentment, or social conflict depending on context and interpretation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Humans often evaluate themselves relative to others rather than in isolation. Envy functions as a comparison-amplification mechanism that highlights perceived gaps between one's current state and the advantages possessed by others. Through envy, status hierarchies become emotionally significant, influencing ambition, consumption, identity formation, competition, aspiration, and social behavior.
