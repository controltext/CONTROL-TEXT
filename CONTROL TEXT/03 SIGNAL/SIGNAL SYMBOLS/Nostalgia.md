SYSTEM [[23 EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The capacity to recognize, understand, imagine, relate to, resonate with, or appreciate the emotions, experiences, perspectives, circumstances, or inner states of another individual.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Creates emotional connection by allowing individuals to model and respond to the experiences of others, facilitating understanding, cooperation, communication, support, compassion, and social cohesion.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Compassionate listening  
- Emotional understanding  
- Perspective-taking  
- Shared grief  
- Shared joy  
- Humanitarian concern  
- Emotional resonance

NOTES:  
  
Empathy does not require agreement, approval, or endorsement. It involves understanding or appreciating another's emotional experience. Empathy may be cognitive, emotional, interpersonal, social, or symbolic.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Humans frequently understand themselves through others and others through themselves. Empathy functions as an emotional-bridging mechanism that reduces psychological distance between individuals. By allowing emotions, experiences, and perspectives to be shared or imagined across boundaries of identity, empathy influences trust, cooperation, communication, persuasion, social bonding, and collective behavior.
