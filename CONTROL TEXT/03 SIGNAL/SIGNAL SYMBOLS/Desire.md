SYSTEM [[23 EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An emotional state characterized by wanting, longing, attraction, craving, aspiration, pursuit, or motivation toward a perceived source of value, pleasure, satisfaction, fulfillment, meaning, achievement, or completion.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Directs attention and behavior toward desired objects, experiences, identities, outcomes, relationships, opportunities, or future conditions.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Desire for success  
- Desire for love  
- Desire for status  
- Desire for recognition  
- Desire for wealth  
- Desire for knowledge  
- Desire for freedom

NOTES:  
  
Desire may be physical, emotional, psychological, social, intellectual, spiritual, or symbolic. Its intensity and direction can vary widely depending on individual values, experiences, beliefs, identities, and circumstances.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention often follows perceived value. Desire functions as an attraction mechanism that pulls awareness and behavior toward imagined sources of fulfillment. By linking present action to anticipated rewards, identities, experiences, or outcomes, desire influences motivation, consumption, ambition, attachment, planning, and long-term behavioral orientation.
