SYSTEM [[19 MANUFACTURED CONSENT]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A selected group of individuals assembled to provide feedback, reactions, interpretations, opinions, attitudes, or perceptions regarding a message, product, idea, policy, narrative, campaign, or communication strategy.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Allows communicators to test, evaluate, refine, and optimize messages by observing how target audiences interpret and respond to information before wider distribution.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political focus groups  
- Marketing focus groups  
- Product testing groups  
- Media testing sessions  
- Advertising evaluation groups  
- Public opinion research groups  
- Narrative testing panels

NOTES:  
  
Focus groups typically involve smaller samples than surveys or polls and prioritize qualitative feedback rather than large-scale statistical measurement. Their value often lies in revealing emotional responses, interpretations, associations, objections, and audience perceptions.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Before messages are distributed widely, they are often tested. A focus group functions as a perception-analysis mechanism that allows communicators to observe how narratives, language, symbols, and messages are received, interpreted, and emotionally processed, enabling communication strategies to be refined before mass deployment.
