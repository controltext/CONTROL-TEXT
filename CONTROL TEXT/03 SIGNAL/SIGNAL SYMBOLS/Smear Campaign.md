SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A coordinated effort to negatively influence public perception of an individual, group, organization, institution, or entity through the dissemination of damaging claims, narratives, accusations, associations, interpretations, or reputational attacks.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Attempts to reduce trust, credibility, legitimacy, influence, status, reputation, or public support by attaching negative meanings and perceptions to a target.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political attack campaigns  
- Reputational attacks  
- Character assassination efforts  
- Coordinated negative media coverage  
- Public discrediting campaigns  
- Social stigmatization efforts  
- Strategic narrative attacks

NOTES:  
  
Smear campaigns may employ accurate information, inaccurate information, selective information, emotional framing, repetition, association, insinuation, or narrative construction. Their defining characteristic is the strategic targeting of reputation and public perception.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Influence can be exerted by altering how a target is perceived. A smear campaign functions as a reputational influence operation that attempts to attach negative associations, interpretations, narratives, or judgments to an individual or group, thereby shaping trust, credibility, legitimacy, and social standing through communication.
