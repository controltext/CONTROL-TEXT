SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A condition in which large volumes of information, messages, reports, narratives, claims, data, media, or communications are introduced into an information environment at a rate or scale that exceeds the ability of individuals or systems to effectively process, evaluate, prioritize, or interpret them.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Overwhelms attention and analytical capacity by increasing informational volume, making it more difficult to identify relevant signals, verify claims, establish priorities, or maintain coherent understanding.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Continuous news cycles  
- Social media saturation  
- Mass content generation  
- Coordinated media campaigns  
- Data overload  
- Information saturation events  
- High-volume communication environments

NOTES:  
  
Information floods may occur intentionally or unintentionally. The information involved may be accurate, inaccurate, relevant, irrelevant, or mixed. The defining characteristic is excessive informational volume relative to available processing capacity.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information environments are shaped not only by content but by quantity. An information flood functions as a saturation condition in which the volume of available information exceeds the ability of participants to effectively evaluate, prioritize, or interpret it, increasing dependence upon shortcuts, narratives, authorities, algorithms, or simplified frameworks for decision-making.
