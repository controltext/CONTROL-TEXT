SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A numerical measure representing the number of individuals who have chosen to subscribe to, follow, monitor, receive updates from, or maintain a connection with a particular person, group, organization, account, or entity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Acts as a visible social signal that communicates audience size, reach, popularity, visibility, attention, or perceived influence within a network.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media followers  
- Channel subscribers  
- Podcast subscribers  
- Newsletter subscribers  
- Fan communities  
- Creator audiences  
- Public account followings

NOTES:  
  
Follower counts indicate audience size but do not necessarily measure trust, expertise, credibility, authority, engagement, or quality. Their influence often derives from visibility, social proof, network effects, and perceived popularity.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Visibility often becomes a proxy for significance. A follower count functions as a social-scale indicator that transforms audience relationships into a simple numerical signal. Through follower counts, attention becomes measurable, popularity becomes visible, and social reach becomes quantifiable, influencing perceptions of importance, legitimacy, influence, and status.
