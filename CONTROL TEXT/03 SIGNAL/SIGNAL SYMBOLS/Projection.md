SYSTEMS [[22 PREDICTIVE PROGRAMMING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process of extending present conditions, trends, assumptions, expectations, models, narratives, beliefs, or observations forward into possible future outcomes, developments, conditions, or realities.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms present information into future expectations by extrapolating existing patterns, trajectories, or interpretations beyond the current moment.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Population projections  
- Economic projections  
- Climate projections  
- Strategic projections  
- Market projections  
- Technological projections  
- Cultural projections

NOTES:  
  
Projections are not guarantees. They are extensions of current assumptions or observed patterns into hypothetical futures. Different assumptions can produce different projections from the same starting conditions.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Humans frequently imagine the future by extending the present. A projection functions as a future-extension mechanism that carries existing patterns, narratives, expectations, and trajectories beyond the current moment. Through projection, present realities become future possibilities, allowing anticipation, planning, interpretation, and strategic action to occur before future conditions emerge.
