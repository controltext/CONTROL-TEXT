SYSTEM [[25 ATTENTION ECONOMY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A group of individuals, participants, observers, viewers, listeners, readers, users, or recipients who are exposed to, consume, interpret, engage with, or respond to a signal, message, communication, platform, creator, or content source.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides the field within which attention exists, circulates, accumulates, and becomes measurable.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Television audiences  
- YouTube audiences  
- Podcast audiences  
- Social media audiences  
- Newsletter audiences  
- Event audiences  
- Streaming audiences

NOTES:  
  
An audience is not necessarily active, unified, or homogeneous. Audiences vary in size, attention, engagement, responsiveness, loyalty, demographics, interests, and behavior.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Signals have no effect without recipients. An audience functions as an attention-reception mechanism that receives, interprets, amplifies, ignores, or responds to information. Through audiences, signals acquire visibility, influence becomes possible, engagement becomes measurable, and attention becomes a resource that can be distributed, retained, converted, and monetized.
