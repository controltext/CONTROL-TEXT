SYSTEM [[25 ATTENTION ECONOMY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process of converting attention, audience participation, engagement, visibility, influence, traffic, signal distribution, or informational activity into economic value, revenue, resources, assets, or financial return.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms attention from a behavioral phenomenon into an economic resource by creating mechanisms through which attention can generate measurable value.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Advertising revenue  
- Subscription revenue  
- Sponsorships  
- Affiliate marketing  
- Donations  
- Premium memberships  
- Merchandise sales

NOTES:  
  
Monetization does not create attention; it extracts value from existing attention. Different monetization models may prioritize different forms of audience behavior such as views, engagement, subscriptions, retention, purchases, or influence.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention becomes an economic asset when mechanisms exist to convert awareness into value. Monetization functions as a value-extraction mechanism that transforms signal activity into resources. Through monetization, views become revenue opportunities, audiences become assets, engagement becomes measurable value, and attention itself becomes a tradable economic commodity.
