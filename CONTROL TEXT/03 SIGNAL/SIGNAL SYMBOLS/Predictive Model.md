SYSTEM [[20 ALGORITHMIC GOVERNANCE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A model, algorithm, framework, or system that uses available information, patterns, historical data, relationships, or inputs to estimate, forecast, predict, or infer future outcomes, behaviors, events, conditions, or probabilities.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Reduces uncertainty by generating expectations about future states, allowing systems to anticipate, prioritize, optimize, recommend, allocate, intervene, or make decisions before outcomes occur.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Recommendation algorithms  
- Demand forecasting systems  
- Risk assessment models  
- Election forecasting models  
- Credit scoring systems  
- Weather prediction systems  
- Behavioral prediction systems

NOTES:  
  
Predictive models do not guarantee outcomes. They generate estimates based upon available information, assumptions, patterns, and methodologies. Their effectiveness often depends upon data quality, model design, environmental stability, and feedback mechanisms.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Governance becomes anticipatory when systems act upon expectations rather than observations alone. A predictive model functions as a future-estimation mechanism that transforms historical and present information into probabilistic forecasts, allowing decisions, recommendations, rankings, and interventions to occur before events fully unfold.
