SYSTEM [[19 MANUFACTURED CONSENT]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A coordinated and intensive communication effort in which a message, narrative, campaign, announcement, product, policy, event, or viewpoint is rapidly distributed across multiple media channels within a concentrated period of time.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Amplifies visibility and attention by increasing the frequency, reach, repetition, and exposure of a message across an information environment.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Election media campaigns  
- Product launch promotions  
- Public awareness drives  
- Crisis communication efforts  
- Political messaging offensives  
- Corporate publicity campaigns  
- Coordinated media saturation events

NOTES:  
  
Media blitzes often employ repetition, message consistency, strategic timing, multi-platform distribution, endorsements, interviews, advertisements, press appearances, and coordinated media coverage to maximize exposure and influence.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Visibility often influences perceived importance. A media blitz functions as an attention-concentration mechanism that increases the prominence of a message through saturation and repetition, shaping public awareness by making a narrative difficult to ignore within the surrounding information environment.
