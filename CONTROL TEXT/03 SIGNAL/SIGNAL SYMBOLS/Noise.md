SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
Information, signals, messages, distractions, distortions, or competing inputs that interfere with the accurate transmission, reception, interpretation, or understanding of meaningful information.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Reduces clarity by competing with, obscuring, overwhelming, distorting, or distracting attention from relevant information within a communication environment.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Information overload  
- Excessive media content  
- Conflicting reports  
- Irrelevant data  
- Communication interference  
- Signal disruption  
- Background distractions

NOTES:  
  
Noise does not need to be false to be disruptive. Accurate, inaccurate, relevant, or irrelevant information can all function as noise if they interfere with the identification, interpretation, or prioritization of meaningful signals.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information environments are shaped not only by what is communicated, but by the volume of competing communication. Noise functions as informational interference that reduces signal clarity by overwhelming attention, complicating interpretation, fragmenting focus, or obscuring meaningful patterns within a field of communication.
