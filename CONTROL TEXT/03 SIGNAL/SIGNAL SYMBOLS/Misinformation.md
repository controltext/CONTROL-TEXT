SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
Information that is inaccurate, incomplete, misleading, mistaken, or false, regardless of whether the person communicating it intends to deceive.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Influences perception, interpretation, belief, and decision-making through the transmission of incorrect or misleading information, often without deliberate malicious intent.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Honest mistakes  
- Misunderstood reports  
- Incorrect statistics  
- Misquoted statements  
- Rumors  
- Misremembered events  
- Unverified claims

NOTES:  
  
Misinformation differs from disinformation because it does not require intent to deceive. Incorrect information may spread through misunderstanding, error, confusion, poor verification, faulty memory, miscommunication, or rapid transmission across networks.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information can influence perception regardless of intention. Misinformation functions as a distortion within an information system where inaccurate content spreads through communication channels, shaping interpretations and beliefs even when no deliberate deception is present.
