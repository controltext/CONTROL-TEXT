SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process of confirming, validating, authenticating, or establishing the identity, legitimacy, authenticity, accuracy, qualification, status, or trustworthiness of an individual, organization, object, claim, credential, or entity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Reduces uncertainty by providing evidence or confirmation that an entity is genuine, authorized, accurate, qualified, or who it claims to be.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Identity verification  
- Account verification  
- Credential verification  
- Document authentication  
- Certification validation  
- Background verification  
- Fact verification

NOTES:  
  
Verification may involve documents, credentials, records, testimony, evidence, authentication systems, institutional validation, technical mechanisms, or social confirmation. The effectiveness of verification often depends upon the credibility of the verifying authority and the reliability of the verification process.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Trust often depends upon certainty of identity and authenticity. Verification functions as a trust-establishment mechanism that transforms uncertainty into confidence by providing evidence that a person, entity, claim, credential, or status corresponds to an accepted standard of legitimacy or authenticity.
