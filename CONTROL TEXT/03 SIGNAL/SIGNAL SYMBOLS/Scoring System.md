SYSTEM [[20 ALGORITHMIC GOVERNANCE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A framework that assigns numerical values, ratings, grades, scores, or evaluations to individuals, groups, objects, behaviors, activities, content, or outcomes according to selected criteria.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms qualitative characteristics, behaviors, attributes, or performances into quantifiable metrics that can be compared, ranked, predicted, optimized, or used for decision-making.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Credit scores  
- Reputation scores  
- Risk scores  
- Engagement scores  
- Performance ratings  
- Social ranking systems  
- Recommendation relevance scores

NOTES:  
  
Scoring systems depend upon the selection of criteria, weighting methods, data sources, evaluation frameworks, and objectives. Different scoring systems may produce different results even when evaluating the same entity.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Algorithms often require simplified representations of complex realities. A scoring system functions as a quantification mechanism that converts behaviors, attributes, relationships, and characteristics into numerical values. Through scoring, systems can compare, rank, reward, restrict, recommend, predict, and govern entities according to measurable criteria.
