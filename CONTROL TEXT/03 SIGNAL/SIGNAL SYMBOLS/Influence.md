SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
SYSTEM [[25 ATTENTION ECONOMY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The capacity to affect, shape, guide, alter, or impact the perceptions, beliefs, attitudes, decisions, behaviors, actions, or outcomes of individuals, groups, organizations, or systems.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms reputation, trust, authority, status, prestige, knowledge, relationships, or visibility into the ability to affect the actions and decisions of others.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social influence  
- Political influence  
- Cultural influence  
- Institutional influence  
- Economic influence  
- Thought leadership  
- Community influence

NOTES:  
  
Influence may arise from expertise, authority, trust, prestige, popularity, relationships, visibility, reputation, resources, or social position. It can be exercised directly or indirectly and may be formal or informal.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Reputation gains power when it affects behavior. Influence functions as the operational output of reputation systems, converting trust, prestige, credibility, and status into the capacity to shape decisions and actions. Through influence, reputation becomes a force that extends beyond perception and begins to alter outcomes within a social system.
