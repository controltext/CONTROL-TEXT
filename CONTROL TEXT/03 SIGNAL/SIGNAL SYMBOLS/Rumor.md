SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An unverified claim, report, story, piece of information, or narrative that circulates through informal communication networks without established confirmation of its accuracy.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transmits information through social communication channels, allowing ideas, claims, interpretations, suspicions, and narratives to spread before formal verification occurs.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Workplace rumors  
- Community gossip  
- Political rumors  
- Market rumors  
- Celebrity rumors  
- Organizational speculation  
- Emerging news reports

NOTES:  
  
Rumors may ultimately prove true, false, partially true, or impossible to verify. Their defining characteristic is uncertainty rather than accuracy or intent. Rumors often spread through social networks, curiosity, emotional relevance, ambiguity, and information gaps.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information often moves faster than verification. A rumor functions as an information packet that spreads through communication networks before its accuracy is established, allowing uncertainty, speculation, interpretation, and narrative formation to influence perception while verification remains incomplete.
