SYSTEM [[23 EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An emotional state characterized by responsibility, remorse, regret, self-reproach, or discomfort arising from the perception that one has caused harm, violated a standard, failed an obligation, broken a commitment, or acted contrary to one's values, beliefs, or expectations.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Directs attention toward perceived errors, violations, responsibilities, obligations, consequences, or harms, motivating reflection, correction, accountability, reconciliation, or behavioral adjustment.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Moral guilt  
- Survivor guilt  
- Parental guilt  
- Professional guilt  
- Relationship guilt  
- Responsibility guilt  
- Failure-related guilt

NOTES:  
  
Guilt differs from shame. Guilt is typically focused on actions, decisions, or behaviors ("I did something wrong"), while shame is often focused on identity or self-worth ("I am wrong"). Guilt may motivate repair, responsibility, growth, avoidance, or self-punishment depending on context.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Humans often regulate behavior through internal emotional feedback. Guilt functions as a self-correction mechanism that links actions to perceived responsibility and consequences. By directing attention toward violations of personal, social, ethical, or relational standards, guilt influences accountability, conformity, reconciliation, behavior modification, and moral decision-making.
