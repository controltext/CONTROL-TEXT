SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A condition of elevated respect, distinction, status, esteem, recognition, influence, or social standing resulting from perceived achievement, reputation, authority, excellence, success, rarity, or accomplishment.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Acts as a social signal that communicates exceptional standing, increasing influence, trust, attention, credibility, desirability, or deference within a system.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Prestigious institutions  
- Elite credentials  
- Distinguished awards  
- High-status positions  
- Respected experts  
- Luxury brands  
- Renowned organizations

NOTES:  
  
Prestige may arise from achievement, exclusivity, history, performance, recognition, influence, scarcity, social consensus, or institutional validation. It often accumulates over time and may persist even after the original achievements that created it.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Not all reputations are equal. Prestige functions as a reputation-amplification mechanism that elevates certain individuals, groups, institutions, or symbols above others within a social hierarchy. Through prestige, reputation becomes status, status becomes influence, and influence becomes self-reinforcing through recognition, attention, and collective belief.
