SYSTEM [[23 EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An emotional state characterized by feelings of inadequacy, humiliation, embarrassment, unworthiness, exposure, rejection, defectiveness, or diminished self-worth arising from perceived failure to meet personal, social, cultural, or moral standards.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Directs attention toward identity, belonging, social standing, perceived flaws, and violations of accepted norms, influencing self-evaluation, conformity, concealment, withdrawal, correction, or transformation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social shame  
- Public embarrassment  
- Moral shame  
- Identity shame  
- Failure-related shame  
- Cultural shame  
- Reputational shame

NOTES:  
  
Shame differs from guilt. Guilt is generally focused on actions ("I did something wrong"), while shame is focused on identity ("There is something wrong with me"). Shame may motivate withdrawal, concealment, conformity, self-improvement, self-criticism, or social repair depending upon context and interpretation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Humans are highly sensitive to social belonging and identity. Shame functions as an identity-regulation mechanism that links perceived social, moral, or personal failures to self-evaluation. By directing attention toward how one is seen by oneself or others, shame influences conformity, reputation management, group membership, self-concept, and behavioral adaptation.
