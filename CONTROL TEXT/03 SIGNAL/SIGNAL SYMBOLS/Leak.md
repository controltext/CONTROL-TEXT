SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The disclosure, release, transmission, or exposure of information that was previously restricted, confidential, private, controlled, or not intended for public distribution.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Alters information environments by moving information from a limited-access context into a wider audience, changing who can access, interpret, distribute, and act upon that information.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Confidential document leaks  
- Internal communications leaks  
- Whistleblower disclosures  
- Data leaks  
- Media leaks  
- Government leaks  
- Corporate information leaks

NOTES:  
  
Leaks may be accidental, intentional, strategic, unauthorized, or sanctioned. Their impact often depends upon authenticity, timing, credibility, distribution, interpretation, and the surrounding information environment.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information derives power not only from its content but from its accessibility. A leak functions as a boundary-crossing event that transfers information from a controlled environment into a broader communication system, potentially altering narratives, perceptions, decisions, relationships, and power structures through changes in information availability.
