SYSTEM [[23 EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An emotional state characterized by the anticipation, perception, expectation, or awareness of danger, threat, uncertainty, loss, harm, vulnerability, risk, or undesirable outcomes.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Directs attention toward perceived threats, increases vigilance, influences decision-making, motivates protective behavior, and prioritizes survival-related responses.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Fear of injury  
- Fear of loss  
- Fear of uncertainty  
- Fear of failure  
- Fear of social rejection  
- Fear of punishment  
- Fear of the unknown

NOTES:  
  
Fear may be proportional or disproportionate to actual conditions. It may arise from direct experience, anticipation, imagination, memory, social influence, narratives, uncertainty, perceived threats, or symbolic representations.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Emotions influence perception before conscious reasoning occurs. Fear functions as an attention-concentration mechanism that rapidly prioritizes perceived threats and uncertainties. By directing awareness toward danger, fear alters interpretation, memory, decision-making, behavior, and social dynamics, making it one of the most powerful emotional forces available for influencing human action.
