SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
Information that is intentionally created, altered, presented, or distributed in a false, misleading, deceptive, or manipulative manner to influence perception, belief, interpretation, decision-making, or behavior.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Attempts to shape understanding by introducing inaccurate or deceptive information into a communication environment, affecting how events, individuals, groups, or situations are perceived.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Fabricated stories  
- False reports  
- Deceptive narratives  
- Forged documents  
- Manipulated evidence  
- Coordinated misinformation campaigns  
- Deliberately misleading communications

NOTES:  
  
Disinformation differs from accidental error because it involves intentional deception. Its effectiveness often depends on credibility, repetition, emotional impact, narrative compatibility, authority, timing, and distribution mechanisms.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information influences perception regardless of accuracy. Disinformation functions as a strategic communication tool that seeks to alter interpretation by introducing false, distorted, incomplete, or misleading representations of reality into an information system, thereby affecting belief formation and decision-making.
