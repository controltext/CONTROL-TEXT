SYSTEMS [[22 PREDICTIVE PROGRAMMING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The presentation of hints, signals, themes, symbols, messages, narrative elements, or suggestions that anticipate, imply, prepare for, or point toward future events, developments, outcomes, or interpretations.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Shapes expectations by introducing future possibilities into awareness before their realization, influencing how later events are perceived, interpreted, remembered, or understood.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Narrative foreshadowing  
- Symbolic hints  
- Early thematic signals  
- Advance narrative cues  
- Story setup elements  
- Future-oriented messaging  
- Anticipatory framing

NOTES:  
  
Foreshadowing does not determine future outcomes. Its defining characteristic is the introduction of anticipatory information that influences how future developments are interpreted when they occur.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Expectations shape perception. Foreshadowing functions as an anticipatory narrative mechanism that introduces future-oriented signals into awareness, creating interpretive frameworks through which later events may appear more understandable, familiar, expected, or meaningful. By placing elements of the future into the present, foreshadowing influences how subsequent information is recognized and interpreted.
