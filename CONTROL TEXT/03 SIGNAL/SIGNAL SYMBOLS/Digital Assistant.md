SYSTEM [[20 ALGORITHMIC GOVERNANCE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A software-based system that assists users by interpreting inputs, processing information, generating responses, providing recommendations, automating tasks, answering questions, or facilitating decision-making through computational methods.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Acts as an intermediary between users and algorithmic systems, translating requests into actions while providing information, recommendations, predictions, automation, and guidance.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- AI assistants  
- Virtual assistants  
- Chatbots  
- Voice assistants  
- Customer service agents  
- Scheduling assistants  
- Recommendation assistants

NOTES:  
  
Digital assistants may operate through predefined rules, machine learning systems, language models, recommendation engines, knowledge systems, or combinations of multiple technologies. Their behavior is influenced by available data, objectives, interfaces, and system design.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Algorithmic systems often require a human-facing layer. A digital assistant functions as an interface mechanism that translates complex computational processes into conversational interactions, recommendations, actions, and guidance. Through this interface, algorithmic governance becomes directly accessible, influencing attention, choices, decisions, workflows, and behavior through ongoing interaction.
