SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process of examining, verifying, evaluating, or assessing the accuracy, consistency, validity, or evidentiary support of a claim, statement, report, narrative, or piece of information.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Attempts to distinguish supported information from unsupported, inaccurate, misleading, or deceptive information by comparing claims against available evidence, records, sources, or verification methods.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Journalistic fact checks  
- Source verification  
- Evidence review  
- Data validation  
- Historical verification  
- Claim analysis  
- Investigative review

NOTES:  
  
Fact checks evaluate claims against available information and evidence. Their conclusions may depend upon source quality, methodology, available records, definitions, context, interpretation, and standards of verification.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information environments contain competing claims, narratives, and interpretations. Fact checking functions as a verification process that attempts to evaluate correspondence between communicated information and available evidence, thereby influencing credibility, trust, interpretation, and belief formation within an information system.
