SYSTEM [[24 SURVEILLANCE SOCIETY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process of capturing, preserving, storing, documenting, or encoding observations, events, communications, activities, behaviors, images, sounds, or information into a durable form capable of later retrieval and review.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms temporary experiences and observations into persistent records that can be archived, analyzed, transmitted, verified, and revisited across time.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Video recordings  
- Audio recordings  
- Security footage  
- Screen recordings  
- Call recordings  
- Body camera footage  
- Event documentation

NOTES:  
  
Recordings may be visual, auditory, textual, digital, analog, automated, manual, temporary, or permanent. Their significance often depends upon accuracy, completeness, accessibility, retention, context, and interpretation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Observation exists in the present. Recording allows observation to survive the present. Recording functions as a memory-preservation mechanism that converts fleeting events into durable information. Through recording, actions become evidence, communications become archives, experiences become histories, and observations become resources available for future analysis, verification, accountability, and governance.
