SYSTEM [[20 ALGORITHMIC GOVERNANCE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured collection of attributes, records, characteristics, behaviors, preferences, relationships, activities, identifiers, and data points associated with an individual, group, object, organization, or entity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms dispersed information into an organized representation that can be analyzed, classified, ranked, predicted, personalized, evaluated, or acted upon by a system.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- User profiles  
- Customer profiles  
- Credit profiles  
- Behavioral profiles  
- Social media profiles  
- Consumer data records  
- Risk assessment profiles

NOTES:  
  
Data profiles may contain demographic, behavioral, transactional, relational, geographic, historical, or preference-based information. Different systems may construct different profiles depending upon their objectives, available data, and classification frameworks.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Governance requires representations of what is being governed. A data profile functions as a digital abstraction that converts an individual or entity into a structured information model. Through this representation, systems can classify, predict, rank, recommend, personalize, optimize, and automate decisions affecting the profiled subject.
