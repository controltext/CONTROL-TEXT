SYSTEM [[24 SURVEILLANCE SOCIETY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
Information that describes, characterizes, organizes, contextualizes, classifies, identifies, or provides attributes about other information, records, events, communications, activities, or data.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms raw observations into structured information by providing contextual details such as who, when, where, how, duration, frequency, relationships, origins, destinations, or classifications.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Call records  
- Email headers  
- GPS timestamps  
- Login histories  
- Communication logs  
- Device identifiers  
- Location records

NOTES:  
  
Metadata may not contain the full content of a communication or event, but it often contains information about participants, timing, frequency, duration, relationships, locations, systems, or patterns. Large collections of metadata can reveal behavioral structures even when content remains unavailable.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Content reveals what happened. Metadata reveals the structure surrounding what happened. Metadata functions as a contextualization mechanism that transforms isolated records into connected networks of relationships, patterns, timelines, and behaviors. Through metadata, systems can reconstruct interactions, map relationships, identify routines, discover anomalies, and generate profiles without necessarily examining the underlying content itself.
