SYSTEM [[25 ATTENTION ECONOMY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The amount of time an audience member spends actively viewing, consuming, observing, or attending to a piece of content, signal stream, media source, platform, or communication.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Measures the duration of attention by quantifying how long a signal successfully maintains audience awareness and engagement.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Video watch time  
- Stream watch time  
- Session duration  
- Audience viewing duration  
- Content consumption time  
- Platform usage time  
- Broadcast viewing time

NOTES:  
  
Watch time measures duration rather than exposure. A signal may generate many views but little watch time if attention is not maintained.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention has both quantity and duration. Watch time functions as an attention-duration mechanism that measures how long a signal remains within awareness. Through watch time, platforms evaluate signal strength, creators evaluate audience interest, algorithms evaluate content performance, and attention becomes measurable not only by occurrence but by persistence.
