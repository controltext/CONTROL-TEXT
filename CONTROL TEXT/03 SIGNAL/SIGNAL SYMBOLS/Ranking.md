SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The process of ordering, prioritizing, sorting, or arranging individuals, objects, content, options, entities, or outcomes according to selected criteria, scores, preferences, metrics, or evaluations.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Determines relative visibility, priority, importance, prominence, or accessibility by assigning positions within an ordered structure.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Search engine rankings  
- Recommendation rankings  
- Product rankings  
- Social media feed rankings  
- University rankings  
- Leaderboards  
- Trending lists

NOTES:  
  
Rankings may be based on popularity, relevance, engagement, authority, performance, quality, reputation, personalization, or other selected metrics. The criteria used to generate rankings significantly influence outcomes.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention is limited while options are abundant. Ranking functions as a visibility-allocation mechanism that determines which entities rise to prominence and which remain obscure by arranging possibilities into ordered hierarchies. Through ranking, systems influence discovery, attention, choice, and behavior by controlling relative position rather than direct access.
