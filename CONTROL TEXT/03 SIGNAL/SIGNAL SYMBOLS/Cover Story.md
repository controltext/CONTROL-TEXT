SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A narrative, explanation, account, identity, justification, or description presented to conceal, obscure, redirect attention from, or protect information, motives, activities, relationships, or events.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Shapes interpretation by providing an alternative framework through which observers understand a situation, thereby influencing attention, perception, inquiry, and explanation.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Operational cover stories  
- Intelligence cover identities  
- Public explanations  
- Alternative narratives  
- Official explanations  
- Strategic justifications  
- Plausible deniability narratives

NOTES:  
  
Cover stories may be truthful, partially truthful, misleading, simplified, strategic, or fictional depending upon their purpose and context. Their defining characteristic is that they provide an explanatory framework that influences how information is interpreted or investigated.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information can be controlled not only by hiding facts but by supplying explanations. A cover story functions as a narrative substitute that directs interpretation toward a particular understanding, reducing attention toward alternative explanations and influencing how events, actions, identities, or situations are perceived.
