SYSTEMS [[22 PREDICTIVE PROGRAMMING]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A hypothetical, imagined, projected, or represented future society, system, world, or condition characterized by perceived social, political, technological, economic, environmental, or cultural dysfunction, oppression, decline, control, suffering, or loss of human flourishing.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Provides a future-oriented narrative framework through which risks, fears, dangers, unintended consequences, and undesirable trajectories can be explored, communicated, interpreted, or warned against.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Total surveillance societies  
- Authoritarian futures  
- Environmental collapse scenarios  
- Technological domination narratives  
- Corporate control futures  
- Social breakdown scenarios  
- Post-apocalyptic worlds

NOTES:  
  
Dystopias are narrative constructions rather than forecasts. They may function as warnings, critiques, thought experiments, symbolic representations, political commentary, cultural reflections, or explorations of possible futures.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Future narratives influence present behavior. A dystopia functions as a cautionary future-model that concentrates perceived risks into a coherent vision of decline or control. By presenting undesirable outcomes as imaginable realities, dystopian narratives shape expectations, fears, planning, interpretation, resistance, and collective understanding of what futures should be avoided.
