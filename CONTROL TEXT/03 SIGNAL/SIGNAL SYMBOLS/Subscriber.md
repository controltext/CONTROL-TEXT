SYSTEM [[25 ATTENTION ECONOMY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An individual, account, participant, or audience member who elects to receive, follow, access, monitor, or maintain an ongoing connection to a source of information, content, communication, media, or signals.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms temporary attention into recurring attention by establishing a persistent relationship between a signal source and an audience member.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- YouTube subscribers  
- Newsletter subscribers  
- Podcast subscribers  
- Streaming subscribers  
- Channel subscribers  
- Publication subscribers  
- Content followers

NOTES:  
  
A subscriber relationship does not guarantee active attention. Subscribers may vary significantly in engagement, activity, loyalty, responsiveness, and frequency of interaction.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention becomes more valuable when it becomes predictable. A subscriber functions as an attention-retention mechanism that converts isolated moments of engagement into ongoing channels of communication. Through subscribers, signals gain continuity, audiences become persistent, and future attention becomes partially secured before content is even produced.
