SYSTEM [[20 ALGORITHMIC GOVERNANCE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A defined sequence of rules, instructions, procedures, calculations, or decision-making processes used to transform inputs into outputs according to specified logic or objectives.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Processes information by applying structured rules that determine how data is evaluated, classified, ranked, predicted, filtered, selected, recommended, or acted upon.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Search algorithms  
- Recommendation algorithms  
- Sorting algorithms  
- Ranking systems  
- Machine learning models  
- Navigation systems  
- Automated decision systems

NOTES:  
  
Algorithms may be simple or highly complex. They can operate through explicit rules, statistical methods, machine learning systems, optimization frameworks, or combinations of multiple approaches. Their outputs depend upon their objectives, inputs, constraints, assumptions, and design choices.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Governance increasingly occurs through procedures rather than direct commands. An algorithm functions as a decision-making structure that transforms information into actions, rankings, recommendations, classifications, and outcomes. By determining how inputs become outputs, algorithms influence what becomes visible, important, prioritized, rewarded, restricted, or acted upon within a system.
