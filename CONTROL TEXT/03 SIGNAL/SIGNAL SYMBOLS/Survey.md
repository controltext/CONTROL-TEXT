SYSTEM [[19 MANUFACTURED CONSENT]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured method for collecting information, responses, opinions, attitudes, experiences, perceptions, preferences, or feedback from individuals or groups through a standardized set of questions.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms individual responses into organized datasets that can be analyzed, interpreted, summarized, and used to understand populations, trends, beliefs, behaviors, or perceptions.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Public opinion surveys  
- Customer satisfaction surveys  
- Market research surveys  
- Employee feedback surveys  
- Academic research surveys  
- Social attitude surveys  
- Demographic surveys

NOTES:  
  
Surveys may collect quantitative or qualitative information. Their results can be influenced by sampling methods, question design, response rates, framing, timing, interpretation, and presentation.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Populations become measurable when responses are systematically collected. Surveys function as information-gathering mechanisms that convert individual experiences, beliefs, attitudes, and perceptions into structured datasets, allowing collective patterns to be identified, interpreted, communicated, and acted upon.
