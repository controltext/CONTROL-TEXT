SYSTEM [[20 ALGORITHMIC GOVERNANCE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An automated system that evaluates, classifies, filters, flags, prioritizes, restricts, approves, removes, or manages content, behavior, interactions, or activities according to predefined rules, policies, models, objectives, or decision frameworks.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Applies governance decisions at scale by continuously monitoring and acting upon information, content, communications, users, or behaviors within a digital environment.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Content moderation systems  
- Automated spam filters  
- Community guideline enforcement  
- Safety classification systems  
- Comment moderation tools  
- Platform trust and safety systems  
- Automated policy enforcement engines

NOTES:  
  
AI moderators may operate through rules, statistical models, machine learning systems, behavioral analysis, risk scoring, classification frameworks, or hybrid human-machine review processes. Their behavior depends upon objectives, training data, policies, thresholds, and governance structures.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Governance becomes scalable when evaluation is automated. An AI moderator functions as an enforcement mechanism that continuously applies classifications, rules, priorities, and restrictions across large information environments. Through automated judgment, visibility, participation, distribution, and access can be managed without requiring direct human review of every action.
