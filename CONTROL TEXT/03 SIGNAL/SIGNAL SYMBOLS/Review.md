SYSTEMS [[21 REPUTATION SYSTEMS]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A written, spoken, recorded, or otherwise communicated evaluation, assessment, opinion, judgment, or account regarding an individual, product, service, organization, experience, behavior, or entity.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms personal experiences and evaluations into publicly accessible reputation signals that influence trust, perception, decision-making, and future interactions.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Product reviews  
- Restaurant reviews  
- Customer feedback  
- Employer reviews  
- Performance evaluations  
- Professional testimonials  
- Service assessments

NOTES:  
  
Reviews may be positive, negative, mixed, detailed, brief, subjective, or evidence-based. Their influence often depends upon visibility, credibility, volume, consistency, recency, and perceived authenticity.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Reputation emerges through communicated judgments. A review functions as a reputation-generation mechanism that converts individual experiences into shared information, allowing trust, credibility, quality assessments, and social evaluations to circulate beyond direct personal interaction.
