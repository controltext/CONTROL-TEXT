SYSTEM [[25 ATTENTION ECONOMY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The quantity of unique individuals, accounts, audience members, or entities that receive, encounter, are exposed to, or have the opportunity to perceive a signal, message, communication, or piece of content.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Measures the distribution and spread of a signal by quantifying how many unique recipients have been exposed to it.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Social media reach  
- Advertising reach  
- Broadcast reach  
- Campaign reach  
- Audience reach  
- Publication reach  
- Content distribution reach

NOTES:  
  
Reach measures how many unique entities were exposed to a signal. It differs from impressions, which may count multiple exposures to the same individual.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention requires access before it can occur. Reach functions as a signal-distribution mechanism that measures how far information travels through an environment. Through reach, signals expand beyond their source, audiences grow, influence scales, and visibility extends across larger portions of a network.
