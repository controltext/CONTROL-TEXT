SYSTEM [[24 SURVEILLANCE SOCIETY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A structured collection of information, records, data, attributes, events, observations, identifiers, or relationships organized for storage, retrieval, analysis, updating, and management.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms individual observations into persistent, searchable, and interconnected information systems capable of preserving and organizing knowledge across time.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Customer databases  
- Surveillance databases  
- Criminal record databases  
- Medical databases  
- Identity registries  
- Financial databases  
- User information systems

NOTES:  
  
Databases may contain structured or unstructured information and can range from simple records to highly interconnected systems. Their significance depends upon data quality, access controls, retention policies, search capabilities, and analytical integration.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Observation becomes enduring when information is preserved. A database functions as an information-retention mechanism that transforms isolated records into organized systems of memory. Through databases, observations accumulate, histories become searchable, identities become traceable, and patterns become discoverable across large collections of information.
