SYSTEM [[18 INFORMATION WARFARE]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A situation in which multiple narratives, interpretations, explanations, accounts, or frameworks compete to define the meaning, significance, causes, implications, or understanding of an event, issue, person, or reality.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Creates a contested information environment where different groups attempt to establish their narrative as the dominant interpretation of events, information, or circumstances.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Political narrative disputes  
- Historical interpretation conflicts  
- Media framing battles  
- Competing explanations of events  
- Ideological disagreements  
- Public perception battles  
- Cultural narrative struggles

NOTES:  
  
Narrative conflicts do not necessarily depend upon deception. Multiple parties may possess different interpretations, priorities, values, assumptions, evidence, or explanatory frameworks. The conflict centers on meaning, interpretation, and perception rather than merely on the existence of information itself.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Information rarely speaks for itself. Narrative conflict emerges when competing groups attempt to organize the same information into different stories, explanations, or interpretations. By shaping meaning rather than merely communicating facts, narratives compete to influence perception, belief, memory, identity, and collective understanding.
