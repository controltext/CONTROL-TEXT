DOMAIN [[THE SIMULATION]]

DEFINITION:  
  
A model, representation, recreation, emulation, or constructed environment designed to imitate, explore, test, analyze, or visualize the behavior of a system, process, event, condition, or possible future state.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Allows individuals and systems to experiment with, rehearse, anticipate, visualize, interpret, or evaluate possible futures before they manifest in reality.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- War games  
- Economic simulations  
- Climate models  
- Disaster preparedness exercises  
- Flight simulators  
- Strategic planning simulations  
- Future world scenarios

NOTES:  
  
Simulations do not predict the future with certainty. They explore how systems may behave under selected assumptions, rules, conditions, variables, or constraints. Multiple simulations may generate multiple possible outcomes.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
The future can be explored before it exists. A simulation functions as a reality-modeling mechanism that transforms abstract possibilities into experiential structures. By creating environments in which future events can be rehearsed, observed, tested, or imagined, simulations allow expectations, strategies, interpretations, and responses to be developed before real-world consequences unfold.
