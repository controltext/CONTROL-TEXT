SYSTEM [[25 ATTENTION ECONOMY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
The measurable interaction, participation, response, involvement, or activity that occurs when an audience member actively responds to, interacts with, contributes to, or participates in a signal, message, piece of content, platform, or communication.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms passive attention into active participation by creating measurable evidence that a signal has generated a response.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Likes  
- Comments  
- Shares  
- Reposts  
- Replies  
- Saves  
- Reactions  
- Discussions

NOTES:  
  
Engagement may be positive, negative, neutral, supportive, critical, emotional, or informational. Most systems measure engagement through observable interactions rather than internal thoughts or opinions.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention becomes economically valuable when it generates activity. Engagement functions as a signal-amplification mechanism that converts awareness into participation. Through engagement, signals gain visibility, algorithms gain feedback, audiences become active, and content acquires measurable influence within information environments.
