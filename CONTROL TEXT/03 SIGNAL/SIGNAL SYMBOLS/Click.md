SYSTEM [[25 ATTENTION ECONOMY]]
DOMAIN [[THE SIGNAL]]

DEFINITION:  
  
An interaction in which an individual selects, activates, opens, follows, or engages with a piece of content, link, object, interface element, message, advertisement, or information source.  
  
FUNCTION:  
  
Transforms passive exposure into measurable engagement by indicating that attention has moved from observation to action.  
  
EXAMPLES:  
  
- Link clicks  
- Advertisement clicks  
- Search result clicks  
- Video selections  
- Product clicks  
- Menu interactions  
- Social media interactions

NOTES:  
  
A click indicates interaction but does not necessarily imply agreement, satisfaction, trust, understanding, or long-term engagement. Different systems may measure clicks differently depending on context and objectives.  
  
CONTROL TEXT OBSERVATION:  
  
Attention becomes economically valuable when it produces action. A click functions as an engagement-confirmation mechanism that signals movement beyond mere exposure. Through clicks, systems gain evidence of interest, curiosity, responsiveness, intent, or participation, allowing attention to be measured not only by visibility but by interaction.
